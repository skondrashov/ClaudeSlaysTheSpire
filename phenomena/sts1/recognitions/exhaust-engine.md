# Exhaust Engine (payoff present or offered)

Applies when: an exhaust payoff is in the deck, on board, or on offer — [[cards/Corruption]], [[cards/Feel No Pain]], [[cards/Dark Embrace]], [[relics/Dead Branch]] — or a mass-exhaust card like [[cards/Fiend Fire]] is in hand.

The questions this situation asks, and what feeds them:

- **How long is this fight, and how long is the run's next fight?** Exhaust front-loads power; a deck that has exhausted its answers plays like a starter deck from there. The long-fight and phased-boss exposures: [[layer:heuristics, exhaust]].
- **What does each Skill actually cost here?** Under Corruption, Skills are free but exhaust. In 4-7 turn fights the turns run net energy-positive even without Feel No Pain (measured, scoped to that fight length); [[cards/Sentinel]] under Corruption is strictly energy-positive — 0-cost block plus its exhaust refund (mechanical, from card text).
- **What taxes the engine?** The engine multiplies Skill *volume*, so per-unit taxes multiply with it — Frail cut generated block ~30% across a measured fight. Count the tax before counting the free cards.
- **Which cards are truly expendable?** Before any mass exhaust, separate Strikes/status/duplicates from debuffs and emergency tools — what exhausts before a phase change or a long fight's late turns does not come back: [[layer:heuristics, exhaust]].
- **Has the payoff actually materialized?** Holding a piece for an engine not yet drawn pays a present cost for a future premise — price the hold against the unblocked damage it costs this turn.
