# Develop Praxis

Improve the Praxis framework based on experience applying it to domains. You are not working in any specific domain — you are refining the framework itself.

## Entry Points

- `ontology/praxis/index.md` — What Praxis is
- `heuristics/praxis/index.md` — Current strategy for using Praxis

## When to Develop Praxis

- After a book has accumulated enough experience to reveal patterns (50+ entries, 10+ goal cycles)
- When a new domain is being set up and the existing Praxis guidance doesn't cover the situation
- When a book-level Curate pass identifies an issue that's framework-level, not domain-level
- When applying Praxis to a second domain reveals assumptions baked into the first

## What to Do

1. **Identify the pattern.** What worked, what didn't, what was surprising. Be specific — name files, quote entries, cite outcomes.
2. **Determine the level.** Is this Praxis-level (applies to any domain) or book-level (specific to this domain)?
3. **Write or update.** Ontology entries for new concepts. Heuristic entries for new guidance. Goal entries if the framework workflow changes.
4. **Ground in experience.** Praxis heuristics should cite the domain experience that informed them, just like domain heuristics cite specific runs.

## Output

- Updated or new entries in `ontology/praxis/` and/or `heuristics/praxis/`
- Assessment of what prompted the change and why
