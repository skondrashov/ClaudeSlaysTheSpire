Hand after Tantrum+ + Rushdown draw: Smite, Defend, Strike, Defend, Empty Body.

**THE CRITICAL ERROR: The player used Distilled Chaos BEFORE playing Smite and Strike.** The Distilled Chaos played the top card of the draw pile, which was Meditate+. Meditate+ enters Calm and ENDS YOUR TURN. The player's turn ended immediately with 0 block against 102 incoming Hyper Beam. Dead.

**Arithmetic verification:** After Tantrum+ dealt 24 to the 21 block + 39 HP boss, BA should be at 39 - (24-21) = 36 HP with 0 block remaining. Smite in Wrath = 12x2 = 24. Strike in Wrath = 6x2 = 12. - Tantrum+: 12 (base, not doubled -- they wrote "NOT doubled by own Wrath entry")

- Strike: 12 (correctly doubled)
- Total: 48

The player miscalculated Tantrum's damage as 12 instead of 24, thought they were 12 short, and used Distilled Chaos to try to make up the difference.

**Root cause: Arithmetic error on Tantrum+ damage in Wrath, leading to unnecessary Distilled Chaos use.**

**Secondary error: Even if the player believed they were 12 short, using Distilled Chaos with Meditate+ in the draw pile (which ends the turn) against 102 incoming Hyper Beam is a game-ending risk.** The player enumerated 5 attacks and 5 skills in the draw pile (50% each). They did not flag that Meditate+ specifically ENDS THE TURN -- it's not just "a useless skill" but an instant death if played. Conclude+ also ends the turn but deals damage first (and might have killed). The Distilled Chaos risk assessment was incomplete.

**Tertiary consideration: Even if Distilled Chaos was needed, it should have been used AFTER playing all guaranteed-value cards first.** Play Smite + Strike for 36 damage, THEN use Distilled Chaos if the boss is still alive. ## Patterns

1. **Tantrum+ Wrath damage ambiguity.** The player was uncertain whether Tantrum+ deals doubled damage when entering Wrath from Calm. On Turn 1 of the boss fight, the player noted Tantrum dealt 12 (not doubled), but on Turn 6 it dealt 24 (doubled). The T1 result may have been affected by Automaton's turn-1 setup (Artifact or block) that the player didn't account for, or there may be a stance-ordering mechanic the player doesn't fully understand. This confusion directly caused the fatal error.

2. **Distilled Chaos risk assessment gap.** The heuristic file for Distilled Chaos says "outcomes vary" and "best when draw pile is stacked with good options." It does NOT warn about turn-ending cards (Meditate, Conclude, Blasphemy) in the draw pile. With Watcher specifically, Meditate+ ending the turn is catastrophic on a kill-or-die turn. This is a heuristic gap.

3. **Potion ordering error.** The player used Distilled Chaos BEFORE playing deterministic damage cards. General principle: play all known-value cards first, THEN use random/variable effects for the remaining shortfall.

4. **Overall execution quality was high.** 32 floors of clean play. The stance dance engine was assembled correctly, Apparitions were timed excellently, shop purchases were optimal, and pathing was well-planned. The death was caused by a single arithmetic error on the kill turn of the Act 2 boss.

4. **Bronze Automaton heuristic is Ironclad-centric.** The current bronze-automaton.md references Bash, Thunderclap, Intimidate, Shockwave, Impervious, and basic Defends. None of these are Watcher cards. A Watcher-specific section is needed covering: Apparition timing vs Hyper Beam, Wrath damage output requirements, and Intangible as the primary Hyper Beam survival tool.

5. **Watcher HP entry threshold for Automaton.** The heuristic says "enter at 70%+ HP." The player entered at 36/36 = 100% (thanks to Pantograph), which is excellent.
