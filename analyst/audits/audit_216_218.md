3. **T3 [event 421]:** "3 attacks on Mystic for Shuriken (+1 Str to 3). 9+9+6=24 into 15 block + 23 HP." Used Thunderclap as one of 3 attacks. The Vulnerable would have increased the player's damage taken by enemies (if enemies were Vulnerable, it doesn't matter -- they take more from player attacks, not deal less).
4. **Death turn [event 436]:** "CRITICAL SURVIVAL: Thunderclap applies Weak (reduces 10x3=30 to 7x3=21). Defend+ (6 under Frail) + Defend (3 under Frail) = 9 block. Take 12. HP 15->3."

**Actual outcome:** Thunderclap applied Vulnerable, not Weak. Centurion's Fury (10x3=30) was NOT reduced. The player took 30 - 9 block = 21 damage at 15 HP = death at -6 HP.

**Why this is uniquely dangerous:** The player had Intimidate in deck (which DOES apply Weak), so the mental model of "mass debuff = damage reduction" was reinforced. But Intimidate exhausts (one use per fight), and Thunderclap is an attack that applies Vulnerable (damage amplification on the target). These are fundamentally different effects.

**Secondary issues:**

### Patterns

1. **Thunderclap/Vulnerable vs Weak confusion (Run 218).** The agent treated Thunderclap as applying Weak (damage reduction) when it applies Vulnerable (damage amplification). This is a card identity error, not an arithmetic error. The math was correct given the wrong premise. This needs a heuristic entry or a correction in the ontology awareness.

2. **No healing cards acquired in any of the 3 runs.** Run 216: no Reaper or Feed offered (unclear from log). Run 217: no Reaper or Feed offered; Sozu eliminated potion healing. Run 218: no Reaper or Feed offered; chose Black Blood for 12 HP/fight (correct alternative). This is partly bad luck (3 runs without being offered the #1 priority card) and partly a drafting attention issue. The playbook says "TAKE IT" for Reaper, but it cannot force the card to appear in rewards.

3. **Potion hoarding until death.** Run 216: Fire Potion, Distilled Chaos, and Snecko Oil all held until death. Fire Potion on T5-T6 would have killed a Sentry and potentially saved the run. Run 218: Strength Potion and Gambler's Brew held until death. Using Strength Potion in the Centurion+Mystic fight would have accelerated the Mystic kill. The combat heuristic says "Use potions when they matter, not hoarded until death." Two runs violated this.

4. **HP threshold violations entering boss/hard fights.** Run 217: entered The Collector at 51% (threshold: 70%). Run 218: entered Centurion+Mystic at 50% (threshold: 60%). Both died. The thresholds exist for a reason -- they represent the damage floor that hard fights impose even with correct play.

5. **Double-end command bug (Run 216).** The agent sent `end` multiple times in the Sentries fight, causing extra enemy turns. This is an interface/command bug, not a strategic error. It needs to be fixed at the harness level.

6. **Bash upgrade timing (Run 217).** Bash was never upgraded in Run 217. The rest-site heuristic says "Bash should be upgraded by Floor 8." The player rested twice (F6, F10) before upgrading Bash via Armaments at F16 (boss fight). This is because HP was always low enough to require resting rather than smithing -- a downstream consequence of the Lagavulin HP drain (56 damage) and lack of healing.

### Flags for Curator

1. **CRITICAL: Add Thunderclap card identity rule to combat.md.** Thunderclap applies Vulnerable (target takes MORE damage), NOT Weak (target deals LESS damage). Only Intimidate, Clothesline, Shockwave, and Uppercut apply Weak among Ironclad cards. This confusion was fatal and could recur. Suggest adding to the Arithmetic Checklist: "VERIFY DEBUFF TYPE: Thunderclap = Vulnerable (50% more damage taken). Intimidate/Clothesline/Shockwave = Weak (25% less damage dealt). These are OPPOSITE effects."

2. **Double-end command bug needs harness fix.** The agent sending `end` twice caused an extra enemy turn in Run 216. This is not a knowledge issue -- it is an interface-level bug that should be prevented by the harness (reject `end` when the turn has already ended or is resolving).

3. **Potion usage heuristic needs strengthening.** The existing heuristic says "use potions when they matter." Two of three runs died with usable potions. Consider adding a trigger rule: "If an elite or hallway fight reaches Turn 5+ and potions are held, evaluate potion use NOW. Do not save potions for 'later' -- there may not be a later."

4. **Sozu risk assessment.** Run 217 took Sozu with no healing card in the deck. The drafting heuristic says "Minimum TWO healing sources by Act 2 Floor 5." Sozu eliminates potions as a healing source entirely. Consider adding: "Do NOT take Sozu unless the deck already has a healing card (Reaper, Feed) or a healing relic (Toy Ornithopter, Meal Ticket). Burning Blood alone cannot offset Act 2 HP drain."

5. **Healing card offering rates.** Three consecutive runs without being offered Reaper or Feed suggests either bad luck or insufficient monster fight count. The drafting heuristic's "Backup Healing Plan" section may need to emphasize shop healing sources more aggressively, or the pathing heuristic should prioritize shops specifically for healing purchases when no healing card has been found by Floor 12.
