ASSESSMENT: error (Normality management)
DETAIL: Took 40 damage (74 to 34 HP). This was the most damaging fight in the run and nearly compromised the boss attempt. The core problem was Normality (curse from Cursed Key, limits to 3 card plays per turn) crippling the Corruption+FNP engine. The engine depends on playing many free Skills per turn to generate block — Normality caps this at 3 total plays.

Specific issues:
- Turn 1: FNP+, Shockwave+, Sentinel = 3 plays (Normality cap). Only 10 block vs ~30 incoming (Weak-reduced). Took ~20 damage.
- Turn 2: Played Seeing Red, Flame Barrier, Defend, Disarm = 4 plays. Wait — Normality limits to 3. The log shows 4 plays were queued, suggesting Corruption may have made the plays free but Normality should still cap at 3. The player may have miscounted or Normality interacted differently than expected.
- Turn 3: Got Corruption+ online (2E). Then exhausted many free Skills for massive block (63 block). This suggests Normality was removed or its interaction with Corruption is different (plays vs cards played).
- Turn 4: Normality re-appeared, limiting to 2 more plays.
- Turn 5: Burning Pact to exhaust Pain, then only Defend for 18 block vs 46 incoming. Took 28 damage.

The decision to exhaust Normality with Burning Pact on the Phase 2 Awakened One turn was smart, but the damage was already done. The Transient fight exposed a weakness: Normality + Corruption+FNP engine is a terrible combination. When Normality limits plays, the engine cannot generate enough block. The player should consider whether Cursed Key is worth the Normality risk in future runs with this archetype, or prioritize exhausting/removing Normality before tough fights.

## Flags for Curator

1. **Normality + Corruption+FNP interaction.** Add a warning to the Cursed Key heuristic or Corruption heuristic: "Normality curse limits card plays to 3/turn. This directly cripples the Corruption+FNP engine which depends on many free Skill plays per turn. With Cursed Key, prioritize exhausting Normality immediately when drawn (Burning Pact, True Grit) or consider whether Cursed Key is the right boss relic for this archetype." The Transient fight cost 40 HP and nearly ended the run.

2. **Brimstone relic.** Add a heuristic entry for Brimstone if one doesn't exist. +2 Str/combat is equivalent to a permanent Inflame that also applies to enemies (+1 Str to them). The tradeoff is almost always worth it for Ironclad, especially with Reaper (more Str = more healing). This run demonstrates Brimstone as a run-defining purchase.

3. **Transient heuristic.** Consider adding a Transient-specific heuristic. Key insight from this fight: Transient has Fading 5, meaning it dies in 5 turns regardless. The optimal strategy is pure block — survive 5 turns, ignore damage. With Corruption+FNP engine, this is trivial UNLESS Normality is in the deck.

4. **Directive 1 data point.** This run used Corruption+FNP WITH Str sources (Brimstone, Spot Weakness). The engine was the primary block generator, but Str was the primary damage source. This does not answer Directive 1 (can the engine win without Str?) but provides another data point that the engine + Str is the proven winning formula.
