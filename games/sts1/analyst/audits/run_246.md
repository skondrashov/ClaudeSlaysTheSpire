## Did the structural fix survive the Transient? YES — and decisively

**The Transient was survived because the player played the mechanic correctly, not because of luck.** Compare to run 245's inversion. At F39 (i728-753) the player:
- Recalled the Transient page (i665) WITH the Maw/Reptomancer/Giant Head Act-3 pool, AND `buffs/Shifting` (i666) — the full awareness manifest the run-245 audit recommended was in context.
- Read Shifting correctly: "ATTACK it hard each turn — Shifting drops its Str by my damage dealt, slashing that turn's incoming" (i728). This is the exact opposite of 245's "SHIFTING = gains block when hit, so DON'T attack it."
- Executed it: T3 Immolate+/2 Strikes = 43 damage dropped the 60 to ~17; T5 Spot Weakness + Heavy Blade+ + Immolate+ = Str -90, reducing the 80 to ~0 under Impervious. Survived 70 -> 39, ~24 net.

**Verdict on the fix:** the `awareness/sts1/boundaries/node-choice.md` Act-3-monster-pool add + the `buffs/Shifting` recall in the Act-3 manifest is what did it — the threat was named AND priced AND its mechanic was loaded at the fight. This is a clean win for the awareness-add half of the run-245 recommendation. The Transient is no longer the open wound. **But it is not evidence that prose discipline fixed routing** — the Transient here was a *forced* monster room the player walked into knowingly and survived on execution, not a fork it priced and avoided.

## D16 finding — the discipline FAILED at the parent node, and it is the 4th corpse of the same family

**Verdict: this run carries a funnel-commit regret. The D16 check fired at SOME forks and lapsed at the ONE that mattered. Run 246 does NOT satisfy D16's success criterion (two consecutive runs with no funnel-commit regret).**

The fatal commit is at the **Act3-F6 Rest fork (game floor 38->39, i726)**. The player wrote: *"Forced F5 Monster (only child) -> F6 fork where I'll take Rest(x2) toward the well-covered Elite(F7) (Rest F6 / Elite F7 / Rest F8)."* The check fired — children were enumerated — but it traced ONE elite (F7) and stopped at the visible "Rest F8," calling the route "well-covered." It did not trace that **F8 Rest's only child is the F9 Treasure, whose only child is the F10 Reptomancer elite, whose corridor leads to the F13 Nemesis elite** — three forced elites, one intervening rest (F8), no rest from F8 until F15.

The full single-child funnel `R(F6.x2)->E(F7)->R(F8)->T(F9)->E(F10)->?(F11)->$(F12)->E(F13)->M(F14)->R(F15)` was **fully visible in the map data at i726.** The alternative **F6 x=6 Rest** branch led to F15 with only ONE forced elite (F7) and many lines hitting ZERO further forced elites (the F10-F13 ?/M rooms gave outs). The player chose the 3-elite corridor over the 1-elite corridor while explicitly reasoning about coverage.

**The damning context: the player demonstrably CAN do this — and did, three times this same run.**
- **i195 (Act1 F11):** traced `Rest->Elite->Monster->Rest->Boss` two levels deep, verified "a rest BEFORE and AFTER the 3rd elite" and that "F15 rest fully resets HP before the Guardian" — then took the elite deliberately. Textbook.

So at the F6 Act-3 fork the player had, one act earlier and twice over, both the trace-two-levels habit AND the avoid-treasure->elite-funnel habit firing correctly. **At F6 it simply lapsed** — counted to the first rest and called it coverage. The capability is present; the application is not reliable across every fork. By i817 (the F9 Treasure, already locked single-child) the 2nd elite is finally named ("it funnels to a forced F10 Elite... I'll handle it"), and the 3rd (Nemesis) not until i851 (after Reptomancer). ## THE CENTRAL QUESTION: promote D10-shape-3 to a maintainer Develop task? — YES.

**Adjudication: prose cannot reliably fix this, and the evidence is now decisive. PROMOTE D10-shape-3 to a maintainer Develop task.**

The reasoning, stated against the strongest counter-argument:

The *competing* hypothesis (prose can fix it) gets its best possible support from THIS run: the player applied the two-level funnel trace correctly THREE times (i195, i490, i143) — proving the prose directive is learnable and executable. A pure "knew better, didn't do it" read would call F6 an isolated execution slip and keep D16 as a habit drill.

That read is wrong, and here is why. The pattern is now **four consecutive deaths to the same funnel family** (243/244/245/246), under escalating prose intervention: D16 was added (244->245), then WIDENED to "price EVERY forced child" (245->246), plus an awareness add. Each cycle the prose got more specific and more correct, and each cycle the funnel killed anyway — at a *different* node each time (rest-child, treasure-child, monster-child, treasure->elite->elite). The failure is not that any one rule is wrong; it is that **a prose rule fires only when the player happens to invoke it, and across ~12 forks per run the one fork where it lapses is the one that kills.** Run 246 is the cleanest possible demonstration: the SAME player, in the SAME run, applied the rule perfectly at three forks and fatally skipped it at a fourth. You cannot patch that with better prose — the prose was already right and already applied elsewhere. The variance is in *invocation*, and invocation is exactly what a tool enforces and a habit cannot.

This is the same lesson D10's own census reached for combat-knowledge delivery: "tool-level fixes stick where habit-level ones don't — choose-N and batch-energy families died only when the tool enforced them." The funnel-commit family is now its routing-layer twin: four corpses, the habit demonstrably learnable yet not reliably invoked, the structural surfacing the only intervention not yet tried.

**Exactly what the tool should surface (D10-shape-3, concrete spec for the maintainer):**

At every MAP-choice node, for each candidate child, the harness already has the full map graph. It should compute and DISPLAY, inline at the choice, for each candidate:
1. **The forced-chain trace** — follow single-child edges forward from the candidate and list every FORCED node until the first genuine branch (>1 child) or the next Rest. Render as e.g. `Rest(F6) -> [FORCED: Elite(F7), Rest(F8), Treasure(F9), Elite(F10)] -> branch(F11)`.
2. **A forced-elite / forced-block-check count to the next rest AND to the act boss**, flagged when >1 forced elite separates two rests, e.g. `WARNING: 3 forced elites (F7/F10/F13), only 1 rest (F8) before F15`.
3. For Act 3 forced-Monster children, the **monster-pool worst case** (Transient block-check / Maw / Reptomancer) tagged on the node, since the player can't know which monster a forced M room rolls.

The point is to put the *count of forced threats between here and recovery* into the state the player reads at the click, so the F8-Rest-is-coverage illusion is impossible — the display would have shown "2 more forced elites after this rest." This is purely a presentation of data the harness already holds; it invents no game knowledge and makes no decision for the player. It is the routing-layer analog of D10-shape-1's combat-start injection.

**Recommended Develop task framing:** "Surface forced-child chains + forced-elite/block-check counts to the next rest at every MAP node." Owner: orchestrator/maintainer (tool layer). This audit is the 4th-corpse evidence that promotes it from D16's problem-statement to an actionable task.

(Note for the directive: D16 itself can now be CLOSED as a prose directive — its hypothesis "pricing every forced child at routing time prevents the commit IF the check fires" has been falsified on the invocation clause; the residual is the tool task above, not a further prose widening.)

## The Nemesis death — routing root + a real in-fight execution miss

The player walked in with eyes open (i873/i877): named the Scythe 45 one-shot risk at 28 HP, the alternating Intangible, planned to burst on non-Intangible turns and Impervious the lethal telegraphs. The page was loaded (Nemesis i666, Shifting/Intangible understood). Execution:
- **T1 (free Scorch, non-Intangible):** Strength Potion + Heavy Blade+ + Thunderclap + 2 Strikes = 78, Nemesis 200->122. Correct max-burst.
- **T2 (Intangible, Scythe 45):** Impervious not in hand; took the GUARANTEED line — Defend+ 9 + Horn Cleat 14 = 23 block, to 6 HP — correctly rejecting an Offering dig as a ~9% instant-death gamble (i882). Good lethal-awareness, correct EV call.
- **T3 (6 HP, 21 Attack):** played **Impervious on the 21 Attack** (i887). THIS IS THE MISS. The page had named the Scythe + alternating Intangible; the player knew T4 would be Intangible (attacks dead) and could carry a Weak'd Scythe. Impervious (30 block, exhausts) was the only tool that covered the T4 Scythe; spending it on a 21 it could have eaten down to a survivable level (with Shockwave+ Weak already cutting future hits) threw away the one card that survives T4.
- **T4 (Intangible, Weak'd-33 Scythe):** only Flame Barrier 12 in hand, Metallicize stranded in pile. 33 - 12 = 21 to a 6-HP player. Dead. The player self-diagnosed it precisely at i888: *"MISPLAY ROOT: I used Impervious on T3's 21 Attack instead of saving it for this T4 Scythe."*

**Diagnosis: EXECUTION error, present + loaded + ignored.** The Scythe and the alternating Intangible were on the loaded page and named in the player's own T1 plan. The Impervious-allocation principle (save the hard counter for the hard-to-block telegraph, eat the small hits) is general lethal-awareness, not a missing fact. No book fix; this is a player-discipline datum (an Impervious/big-block ALLOCATION miss under an alternating-Intangible clock). Worth noting that even perfect T3 allocation only *might* have saved it — at 6 HP behind a 33 recurring Scythe with the burst stranded behind Intangible turns, the fight was ~75% lost on entry. The allocation miss converted a ~20% survival into a ~0%; the 28-HP entry is where the real win% went.
