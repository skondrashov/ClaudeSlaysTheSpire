## 5. turn() "auto-end" claim — VERDICT: MISREAD, NOT A BUG

The player logged (idx 400): "turn() AUTO-ENDS even without 'end' in batch — interface friction, cost ~8 HP on the Fell turn." **Refuted on three independent grounds:**

1. **The misplay turn's own command contains the end.** The Shelled Parasite turn that cost the HP (T3, idx 367-369: hp 69→57 through Fell) ended via the player's own batch `play Shrug It Off; end` — the `end` is in the logged command, and turn() logs the action list verbatim (`"; ".join(actions)`, cmd.py). Likewise idx 379, where the reasoning says "sending without end" — the logged command is `play Defend; play Defend; play Feel No Pain; end`.
2. **Code:** cmd.py's `turn()` executes exactly the actions passed; no code path injects an `end`. The draw-stop guard skips remaining actions after a mid-batch draw **except when the next action is exactly `"end"`** (cmd.py ~1845) — i.e., a trailing `end` after a draw card is deliberately honored. That exception is the real mechanism: batching `play SIO; end` commits to ending before seeing SIO's draw, forfeiting the drawn card and any remaining energy.
3. **In-run counter-example:** idx 411 (`play Bash 0; play Strike 0; play Strike 0` — no end) did NOT end the turn; the player issued a separate `end` at idx 412.

So: **player-included end, amplified by not knowing the draw-stop's end-exception.** Cost ~8-12 HP. The false bug note must NOT be promoted anywhere. The real, fix-shaped lesson (filed in observations): never batch `end` immediately after a draw card while energy remains — combat.md's draw rule currently blesses exactly that shape ("it MUST be the last card before 'end'") and should be amended; alternatively a tool-level warning (the guard family that retired the energy-overdraw errors) on `[...draw card, "end"]` batches with energy left.
