## Run 220

**Ironclad A5 | DEFEAT (Sentries) | Floor 6 | HP 0/80**

Early death. Neow -24 HP start, Acid Slime L drained HP, forced into elite at 12 HP with no potions.

### FLOOR 0 -- Neow Blessing

DETAIL: Took -24 HP for a rare card choice. Got Offering (strong card, but not a MUST-TAKE tier rare like Reaper/Immolate/Impervious). Starting at 56/80 (70%) is risky when the route requires surviving fights before any rest site. The decision was defensible -- rare card gambles have high upside -- but the 24 HP cost was steep given the map.

### FLOOR 1 -- Cultist
ASSESSMENT: correct
DETAIL: Clean kill in 2 turns. Used Offering on the free Incantation turn to draw 5 cards and set up a 5E burst (Bash + 3 Strikes = 35 damage). Kill on Turn 2 with 2 Strikes. Zero damage taken. Took Smoke Bomb (potion) and Metallicize (block scaling gap). Good decisions.

### FLOOR 2 -- 2x Lice
ASSESSMENT: correct
DETAIL: Used Offering to draw attacks. Set up Metallicize on Turn 1. Took only 1 damage (Defend + Metallicize = 8 block vs 9 incoming on Turn 2). Took Weak Potion and Thunderclap (AOE gap, HIGH PRIORITY tier). Correct pick.

### FLOOR 3 -- Jaw Worm
ASSESSMENT: correct
DETAIL: Took 12 damage on Turn 1 (no block cards in hand, all-attack opening). Correctly played for kill speed vs Jaw Worm scaling. Kill math on Turn 3 was exactly lethal (3 Strikes x 6 = 18 vs 6 block + 12 HP). Correctly did NOT include "end" with the kill attempt per combat checklist rule #2. Skipped card reward (True Grit unupgraded, Blood for Blood, Warcry -- correct skip). Took Duplication Potion.

### FLOOR 4 -- Event (Wing Statue)
ASSESSMENT: debatable
DETAIL: Removed a Strike for 7 HP (36 HP remaining, 45%). Deck thinning is correct per heuristic. However, at 45% HP headed toward an elite, spending 7 HP is risky. The player's reasoning ("going to 36/80 is acceptable with 3 potions as insurance") was sound at the time. The problem is the cumulative HP drain: Neow -24, Jaw Worm -12, Wing Statue -7 = 43 HP lost before any elite.

### FLOOR 5 -- Acid Slime (L)
ASSESSMENT: error (routing)
DETAIL: The player chose Monster at x=0 knowing it led to Elite at F6 x=0. The routing plan was "F5 Monster -> F6 Elite -> F7 Unknown -> F8 Rest." At 36 HP, the plan depended on the Monster fight being cheap. Acid Slime (L) at A5 was not cheap. Turn 1: Bash + Defend (8 dmg + 5 block), took 18 from Tackle. HP dropped to 23 (28%). Used Weak Potion correctly (potion timing rule: HP below 30%). Turn 4 split into 2x Medium Slimes dealing 20/turn combined. Had to Smoke Bomb at 6 HP to escape.

The routing error was choosing F5 Monster at 36 HP when the F5 path committed to F6 Elite with no rest between. The alternative was F5 Unknown (x=1) which also led to F6 Elite (x=0). The F5 Shop (x=2) led to F6 Rest (x=2). The player should have taken the Shop path at x=2 to reach the Rest site at F6 instead of the Elite. This would have preserved HP and avoided the forced elite at 12 HP.

Good execution during the fight itself: Weak Potion at HP<30%, Smoke Bomb escape when survival was impossible. But the routing decision upstream caused the crisis.

### FLOOR 6 -- 3x Sentries (Elite, Death)
ASSESSMENT: error (card index confusion)
DETAIL: Entered at 12 HP, no potions. The player correctly identified this as likely fatal. On Turn 1, the player sent the command `play Thunderclap; play Strike 1; play Defend; end` but this was NOT what executed. The next command was `play 1` with reasoning "Play Thunderclap (index 1) to strip Artifact" -- but index 1 was Metallicize, not Thunderclap (hand was: [Thunderclap, Metallicize, Strike, Strike, Defend]). The result shows Metallicize was played (no damage to Sentries, 2E remaining). Then `play 1` again with reasoning about Metallicize, but executed Strike. Then `play 3` (Defend).

The initial batched command with card names failed to execute (possible interface issue), and the player fell back to indices without verifying card identity. This violates combat checklist rule #5 ("VERIFY CARD IDENTITY BEFORE PLAYING") and rule #6 ("USE CARD NAMES IN turn() BATCHES -- NEVER INDICES"). The player had already lost by this point (12 HP vs 3 Sentries with no potions), but the card index confusion compounded the problem.

**Root cause chain:** Neow -24 HP -> Jaw Worm -12 -> Wing Statue -7 -> Acid Slime L at 36 HP -> Smoke Bomb at 6 HP -> forced Elite at 12 HP -> death. The routing error at F5 was the actionable mistake. The card index error on F6 was secondary (death was near-certain regardless).

Reached Act 3 at 98 max HP (Feed+ gains).

### Act 3 Boss -- Awakened One (FLOOR 50, Death)
ASSESSMENT: correct (execution was clean; death was unavoidable on the lethal turn)
DETAIL: This fight lasted 11 turns across 2 phases. Key execution points:

**Phase 1 (correct):** The player correctly avoided playing Inflame+ in Phase 1 (Curiosity: AO gains Str per Power played). This is correct Awakened One knowledge. Played damage and block efficiently to close Phase 1.

**Phase 2, Turns 9-11:**
- Turn 9: AO at 300 HP, 42 incoming (Dark Echo 40+2 Str). Player at 25 HP, 19 Str, 4E. Exhaustive analysis of 8+ options in think(). Chose Clothesline (Weak, 31 dmg) + Flame Barrier (12 block). With Weak: 42*0.75=31 incoming, 12 block, take 19. Survive at 6 HP + 4 Regen = 10 HP. **Correct: best survival line.**

- Turn 10: AO at ~272 HP, 15 incoming (Sludge). Player at 10 HP, 19 Str, 4E. Played LB+ (Str 19->38) + 3x Defend (15 block = exactly incoming). Full block, take 0. Survive at 10 + Regen = 13 HP. **Correct: perfect play.** However, Sludge adds Void to the deck. The player noted this: "Sludge adds Void to deck (unplayable, lose 1 energy when drawn)."

- Turn 11 (DEATH): AO at 279 HP, 36 incoming (Tackle 12x3). Player at 13 HP, 38 Str, **3E** (not 4E). The Void card was drawn at start of turn, draining 1E (4->3). With only 3E, max block was SiO(1E, 8) + Flame Barrier(2E, 12) = 20 block. Tackle: 12+12+12=36. First hit: 12 vs 20 block = 8 block remaining. Second hit: 12 vs 8 = 4 damage through. Third hit: 12 damage through. Total: 16 damage. 13 + 2 (Regen) - 16 = -1 HP.

**With 4E (no Void):** SiO(1E) + Flame Barrier(2E) + Defend(1E) = 25 block. 36-25=11 damage. 13+2-11 = 4 HP survived. The player would have survived and next turn had HB+ at 38 Str for 204+ damage, easily lethal on AO at 279.

**Void was the difference between winning and losing by exactly 1 HP.** The player's analysis on the death turn was thorough and correct -- no alternative line survives. The exhaustive 1000+ character think() verified every combination. Execution was flawless.

**No execution error.** This death was caused by the Void status card mechanic (energy drain on draw), which the player acknowledged but could not prevent. The Sludge attack on Turn 10 was unavoidable (had to block it or die). The Void drawn on Turn 11 was RNG (deck shuffle placed it in the opening hand).

**Potential earlier alternative:** Could the player have killed AO before Turn 11? On Turn 10, instead of LB+ + 3 Defends, the player could have gone all-damage: Bash+(2E, 29+Vuln) + 2 Defends (2E, 10 block), taking 5 damage (10-5=5 HP, then +Regen=8 HP). AO at 272-29=243, Vulnerable. Turn 11: HB+ at 19 Str with Vuln = (14+95)*1.5 = 163. AO at 243-163=80 HP. Not a kill. Would need another turn. And at 8 HP vs 36 incoming (Turn 11) with only 3E (Void) -- same death. The LB+ line was actually better: it set up a one-shot kill next turn (HB+ at Str 38 with Bash Vuln = 306 damage > 279 HP). The player just needed 1 more energy on Turn 11. RNG denied it.

### Patterns

**Routing errors are the #1 killer (2 of 3 deaths).** Both Run 220 and Run 222 died because the player committed to a path that required surviving an elite without rest site coverage. In Run 220, the F5 Monster path locked into F6 Elite at 12 HP. In Run 222, the F7->F10 elite-to-elite path had no rest between. This is the same failure mode identified in audit_216_218 (Run 217: forced resting in Act 2 due to lack of healing). The heuristic for route planning needs to treat rest site coverage before elites as mandatory, not optional.

**Void status card is a new knowledge gap.** Run 221's death by exactly 1 HP due to Void energy drain is a new mechanic interaction. The player correctly identified and documented this. Void (from Sludge) drains 1E on draw. With Runic Pyramid retaining cards, the hand was already large; drawing Void on the lethal turn removed the 1E that would have provided the 21st point of block needed to survive. No playbook entry exists for Void management.

**Lack of Str scaling kills runs (Run 222).** Run 222 had Immolate (burst AOE) but no Strength source. Against Lagavulin (110 HP elite with Siphon Soul debuffs), base damage was insufficient. The Act 2 Readiness Checklist requires "damage scaling" but the player entered the second elite without any. The deck was lean (13 cards) and clean but lacked the scaling to handle high-HP elites.

### What Went Right

1. **Run 221 was nearly a win.** Died by 1 HP to AO Phase 2. The deck was excellent, the execution was flawless, and the fight analysis was extraordinarily thorough (1000+ character think() exploring 8+ options on the death turn). This run demonstrates that the Ironclad A5 strategy is sound -- the death was RNG (Void drawn on the lethal turn).

2. **Potion timing was correct in all 3 runs.** Run 220 used Weak Potion at HP<30% and Smoke Bomb for emergency escape. Run 222 used Colorless Potion and Blessing of Forge in desperation on the lethal turn. No potions were hoarded until death.

3. **Card name usage in batched commands.** Run 220 attempted to use card names in the initial batch command (correct per rule #6), though the batch failed and fallback to indices caused an error. Runs 221 and 222 used card names correctly throughout.

4. **Kill math verification.** Run 220 correctly separated kill attacks from "end" per rule #2 (Turn 3 Jaw Worm). Run 222 did the same on Nob Turn 4.

5. **Fight strategy planning.** All 3 runs produced fight strategy think() blocks before every elite and boss. Run 221's exhaustive analysis on the death turn was exceptional -- every possible play combination was evaluated with arithmetic.

6. **Drafting was sound.** Run 221 assembled an excellent deck satisfying all 4 checklist criteria. Run 222 correctly took Immolate (MUST-TAKE) and Shrug It Off (MUST-TAKE). Run 220 correctly took Thunderclap (AOE gap) and Metallicize (block scaling). No drafting errors identified.

### Curator Flags

1. **FLAG: Route safety heuristic needed.** Two of 3 runs died from routing into elites without rest coverage. The playbook mentions "path through more rest sites" under Backup Healing Plan and "conservative pathing" in the Act 2 Readiness Checklist, but there is no explicit rule requiring rest site access before/after every planned elite. Proposed rule: "Never route through an elite unless at least one rest site is reachable within 2 floors after the elite, OR HP is above 70% and no second elite follows before a rest."

2. **FLAG: Void status card not documented.** Void drains 1E when drawn. With Runic Pyramid (cards retained in hand), Void persists and drains energy every turn it is drawn. No heuristic exists for Void management. Proposed addition to a matchup or status card reference: "Void: When drawn, lose 1 energy. Cannot be played. Added by Sludge. With Runic Pyramid, Void stays in hand but does NOT drain energy again (only on draw). Key risk: energy loss on critical survival turns."

3. **FLAG: Damage scaling gap check before elites.** Run 222 entered Lagavulin with no Str source. The Act 2 Readiness Checklist checks for scaling by Floor 15, but the player committed to 2 elites by Floor 10 without meeting criterion #1. Proposed refinement: "Before routing through Act 1 elites, verify the deck has at least one damage scaling source (Inflame, Spot Weakness, Demon Form, or a strong Uncommon attack like Carnage). Without scaling, high-HP elites (Lagavulin 110 HP, Sentries 40+42+42) are DPS races that base-damage decks cannot win."
