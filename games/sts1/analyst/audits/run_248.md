## §1 — ROUTING TOOL vs VARIANCE (the key question)

**Verdict: the routing tool plausibly moved the failure mode earlier; this is more than seed variance.** Caveat n=2, but the evidence is mechanistic, not just outcome-shaped.

**The tool is live and confirmed in code.** `state_formatter.py::_route_analysis` (lines 456–508) renders a `FORCED-ROUTE ANALYSIS` block on the MAP screen: per choosable node, the forced single-child chain to the next rest, with flag strings `⚠⚠ N FORCED elites before any rest`, `⚠ forces an elite with no rest before it`, and `⚠ forces an Act 3 monster room (price its worst case)`. It is presentation-only and printed live — it is NOT stored in the run JSON's `map` state (the JSON keeps raw nodes/children only), which is why grepping the log for the tool's output finds nothing; you find it instead in the player's routing prose.

**The player consumed it at every fork, in both runs.** The tool's verbatim flag vocabulary appears throughout the routing thinks:
- 248 F10 (i793): "F10 Elite(x3)→[4] has **NO rest after (flagged)**" → chose Monster to keep F11 Elite WITH F12 Rest coverage.
- 248 F11 (i848): at 22/85, "SKIP the elite … take Monster(x3) which reaches F12 Rest(x3)" — rest-coverage reasoning straight off the tool.
- 247 F5 (i163): "[0] Shop→**FORCED Elite at 42HP (⚠ no rest)**, [1] Monster→REST→Elite" — the `⚠ no rest` is the tool's literal flag string; the player routed around the funnel.
- 247 F9/F13 (i318/i383): explicit "FUNNELS into", "funnel trap", forced-chain enumeration before each click.

**Neither 247 nor 248 carries a funnel-commit regret.** This is the exact thing 243/244/245/246 all died to (D16: "the run's largest avoidable regret was the same shape — a node committed past a funnel whose forced child killed the run at low HP"). In 247 and 248 the deaths are at the **act boss**, off a rest-vs-smith / prep decision — not a forced child the player walked into blind. **That satisfies D16's success criterion** ("Two consecutive audited runs with NO funnel-commit regret of EITHER kind, the check having fired and priced the worst-case forced child at the fork"). The maintainer's bet — that only a TOOL-level surfacing at the node-choice boundary would bind where habit-level recitation didn't (D10 shape 3, "third corpse") — looks vindicated on this n=2 window.

**Honest counter-weight (the variance caveat):** 247 and 248 are different decks (247 a thin, scaling-light Carnage deck that died F16; 248 a strong Snecko/Reaper burst deck that died F33). Different decks dying at different depths is partly seed/draft variance, and a 2-run window cannot rule it out. But the *shape* of the deaths changed in a way variance does not explain: the funnel-commit regret — present in four straight runs — is **absent** in both post-tool runs despite both running through Act-2 corridors with forced elites, and in both the player's routing prose cites the tool's flags by their literal strings. The cleanest reading: the tool removed the funnel-commit death; the new ceiling is boss prep, which the tool does not touch. **This is the finding to take to Curate** (resolve D16, open the boss-prep doctrine — §2).

## §2 — BOSS-PREP DOCTRINE: the rest-vs-smith blind spot (steepest regret)

**FLOOR 32 — Pre-Champ rest site. ASSESSMENT: ERROR (the steepest regret of the run). REGRET: large — this is the run-deciding decision.**

The player smithed Bash+ at 48/85 and entered the Champ at 56%, reasoning (i972): *"56% — ABOVE 50%, so the doctrine favors UPGRADE over rest."* This is a **misapplication of a cached threshold the book itself disavows.** Two book facts collide here:

- `heuristics/sts1/hp-management.md` line 12 (Act 2 prep): *"upgrade over rest when above 50%"* — a cached entry-threshold.
- The SAME file, lines 5–6 ("Pathing is a ledger you price LIVE"; *"Why not entry thresholds: a rule like 'enter above 60%' … throws away your deck, the rest of the path, and your recovery options"*), and `rest-sites.md` line 3 (*"Rest when current HP doesn't cover that sum with margin for a spike; otherwise upgrade"*).
- `heuristics/sts1/bosses/the-champ.md` line 12: *"HP management: Budget for an expected 30-50 HP fight — run the ledger before the final rest site decision."*

The Champ-specific page told the player to budget **30–50 HP** for this fight and run the ledger. At 48 HP that ledger does **not** clear — and the Champ is not a flat-damage boss where a budgeted 30–50 is survivable from 48. Its Phase-1 loop **erases block** (Face Slap = 2 Frail [block ×0.75] + 2 Vulnerable [incoming ×1.5], verified against `ontology/sts1/bosses/the-champ.md`). Against a deck whose only block was Flame Barrier + Shrug + Bronze Scales (no passive/repeatable block), the Frail stack shrinks every Defend while Vulnerable inflates every hit — a compounding spiral the 30–50 budget does not capture, because that budget assumes you can *block* the budgeted damage. You can't, under Frail.

The death trace bears this out exactly: T6 (i1034) 9 HP with Frail 2 cutting block; T7 (i1044) 19 incoming vs a 6-block-after-Frail ceiling = lethal — with the Champ still at ~302/440, **deep in Phase 1**. The player never reached the Anger/Execute Phase-2 threat the prep was oriented around. **The killer was P1 debuff-stacking, not P2 burst.**

**The codifiable rule (knowledge gap — for Curate):** the ">50% → upgrade" cached threshold has a real blind spot against **debuff-stacking bosses that erase block** (The Champ's Frail+Vuln; Time Eater's similar attrition shape). For these, the live ledger must price the *spiral*, not the nominal attack table: under Frail your realized block ceiling is ~75% of nominal and falling, and under Vuln the spikes are ×1.5 — so the effective fight cost for a thin-block deck is well above the 30–50 stored figure. The player's proposed "rest if below ~65% vs Champ" is directionally right and a reasonable cache, but the *correct* framing (consistent with the book's anti-threshold doctrine) is: **vs a block-erasing debuff boss with no passive/repeatable block in deck, the ledger prices Frail-reduced block against ×1.5 spikes — which for a thin-block deck means rest unless near-full.** Note this is a Curate flag, not an execution error: the player followed a threshold that *was* in the book (hp-management line 12) and was not loaded with the contradicting ledger framing for *this specific boss shape* at the rest site. Borderline knowledge-gap (the spiral interaction is not spelled out) plus a latent book contradiction (the cached threshold vs the live-ledger doctrine).

**Where the HP actually went (walking the trajectory back, per audit method):** the 48-HP entry is the residue of a healthy-but-not-full Act 2: Parasite+Fungi cost ~39 (55→16, F27), Slavers/Centurion+Mystic elites ran 57→21 (F22–24), and the F21 Forgotten Altar Sacrifice cost a clean 20 (66→46). None of these were misplays — the Parasite armor-strip line (Chemical-X Whirlwind) and the Slavers race were correct. The run was simply a normal Act-2 grind that landed the player at 48 at the rest site. The regret is **not** "entered too low"; it is the rest-site decision to *smith instead of heal* given that 48 against a block-eraser does not clear the ledger. The F32 rest was the lever, and it was pulled the wrong way.

## §4 — UI misplays (classification + interface recommendation)

**F1 card reward — `choose 0` took Whirlwind, intended index 1 Spot Weakness. CLASSIFICATION: execution error ON TOP OF an interface gap.** The index did exactly what it said (no desync — distinct from IB-003/IB-013). But the card-reward screen is the **one selection screen that does not advertise by-name choosing**: `format_card_reward` (state_formatter.py ~line 619) ends `Use: choose <index> to take` — index only — while the shop (line 412, "Buy by NAME (preferred) … choose purge") and rest (line 596, "choose <option_name>") both surface and recommend names. CommunicationMod accepts card names at CARD_REWARD, so the affordance exists at the engine level; it is simply not shown, leaving an index fumble with no name-based safety net on a high-leverage, hard-to-undo pick. **Filed as IB-017** with a cheap fix (add the by-name hint to `format_card_reward`). The player owns the slip; the UI gave them no better tool to reach for.

**F20 shop — `choose 0` bought Wild Strike, intended a 2nd card removal (removal index shifted after the first purge). CLASSIFICATION: execution error; existing name support is SUFFICIENT — no new guard warranted.** This is the IB-003 *shape* (post-purge re-indexing), but the fix already shipped and was not used: the first removal at i619 was done correctly, and `_build_shop_choice_list` exposes `purge` as a stable name, with `format_shop` line 412 explicitly printing *"Buy by NAME (preferred): choose <Name> (e.g., choose Inflame, **choose purge**)."* The player used a raw `choose 0` for the second removal instead of `choose purge`, and the translated echo even read "Buy Wild Strike" before commit. **By-name enforcement is already present and recommended in the UI; the player ignored it.** No guard or by-name addition is warranted here — the existing name support is sufficient and this is pure execution. (Curate-worthy as a *habit* flag, not an interface flag: "at shops, buy removal as `choose purge`, never a raw index — the index shifts after every purge.")
