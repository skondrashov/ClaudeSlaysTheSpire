## Steepest-regret decisions (ranked)

### 1. Hexaghost: declined cheap Wrath bursts to avoid doubling a small Sear — regret ≈ 6–8 pts (the real loss)
**FLOOR 16 · idx 306–307 (and the analogous Calm-hold turns 319, 336) · the one-turn deficit**

This is the player's own post-mortem and it is **correct**. Concretely, the cleanest missed burst:

The player chose "stay neutral, don't double Sear": Strike 6 + Pressure Points 8 = **14 chip**, blocked the Sear, held Eruption+. The available Wrath line: **Eruption+ (1E, enter Wrath, deals 9) → Strike ×2 = 12 → Pressure Points 8 = 29 damage** (109→80) and simply eat the doubled Sear 16 (40→24). That is **+15 boss damage for 8 net HP** at 40 HP, in a race the player lost by ~one boss turn. In a boss fight (combat.md Exception #1: *spend HP freely*) where HP fully resets next act, 8 HP to shave a whole turn off a 250-HP race is correct.

The same pattern repeats at **idx 319** (boss 87, player 29, Sear 8 — Crush Joints 8 + block, held Eruption+ "for next turn") and **idx 336** (free Inflame turn used for only Empty Fist 9 + Vigilance, no Eruption+ burst because it wasn't drawn). Eruption+ was held "for a free/exit turn" on idx 306, 309, 311 — three consecutive turns — while the deck filled with Burn+ from each Sear/Inferno. By the time the player committed, the draw was clogged (6 Burn+) and the lethal turn arrived with only 2 Strikes (12) against a 20-HP boss + a 36 Inferno.

**Counterfactual:** front-loading even one of these Eruption+ bursts (idx 307 or 319) puts the boss ~12–17 HP lower entering the final Inferno turn — i.e., into Pressure-Points-plus-Strike lethal range, or low enough that the player blocks the prior turn instead of racing. **This is the difference between the observed 14-HP-survivor loss and a win.** Classification: **execution error against loaded guidance** — combat.md #9 (Wrath doubles) and Exception #1 (bosses: spend HP) were both available; the player over-applied the hallway "preserve HP / don't double the incoming" instinct inside a boss race. Good calibration (the player *named* the error live at idx 357), so this is an EXECUTION flag, not a book hole.

## The Wrath-burst codification question (verdict)

The human asked where the Spike-Slime synthesis should live: (a) a Spike-Slime heuristic, (b) a general "Wrath in hand + burst-down-able threat → burst now" **phenomenon**, or (c) a general Watcher rule that Wrath's incoming-damage cost is moot on a lethal/burst turn.

**Verdict: (b) as the primary home, with (c) as the underlying rule it cites. Reject (a) as the primary home.**

Reasoning from this run's evidence: the *same* failure mode appeared in **two different fights** — the Spike Slime split (idx 192, "out of Wrath for safety" with zero incoming) **and** the Hexaghost race (idx 307/319, declining Eruption+ bursts to avoid doubling a 6–8 Sear). A Spike-Slime-specific heuristic (a) would not have fired in the boss fight, which is where the run was actually lost. The unifying trigger is general: **you hold a Wrath entry + attacks, and the incoming damage you'd double is either zero (a non-attacking/splitting/setup turn) OR small relative to the win% you gain by front-loading the kill (boss race, lethal, or a splitter you can minimize) → enter Wrath and dump all attacks (and any free-energy cards like Miracle) THIS turn.**

So:
- **(c) is the true rule** and should be stated plainly in a Watcher combat/stance heuristic: *Wrath's 2× incoming cost is only a cost on a turn where you take a hit you can't afford. On free turns, setup/split turns, lethal turns, and boss races where HP resets, the cost is moot or worth paying — burst in Wrath.* This generalizes the existing combat.md Exception #1 (bosses: spend HP) to the stance system.
- **(b) is the actionable trigger** that makes (c) fire at the table — and it should be a **phenomenon/survey entry** precisely because survey returned "nothing flagged" here when it most needed to. Trigger condition: *Watcher holds a Wrath-entry card AND faces (i) a splitter at/near its split threshold, or (ii) an enemy whose next-turn damage you can prevent by killing/minimizing it, or (iii) a boss race with HP to spare → enter Wrath, front-load all attacks + free-energy cards now.*
- **(a)** the Spike-Slime files should get only a one-line *cross-reference* to (b) ("at the split threshold, a Watcher should burst in Wrath to minimize spawn HP — see the Wrath-burst phenomenon"), not be the canonical home.

The player "had all the components but failed to synthesize," and they *named the same miss in the boss fight live* — so the codified form must make the **trigger explicit and general**, not bury it in one enemy's file. (b)+(c) does that.

## Patterns

1. **Over-applying the hallway "preserve HP / don't double the incoming" instinct inside a boss race.** Both the Spike Slime and Hexaghost misses are the same reflex: refuse Wrath to avoid a doubled hit, even when the hit is zero (split turn) or trivial (Sear 6–8) and the win% is in the burst. combat.md Exception #1 already licenses spending HP in bosses; the player didn't extend it to the stance decision.
2. **Holding the enabler "for next turn."** Eruption+ was held across idx 306/309/311 and Miracle across idx 190–192. In a race, deferring the burst let the Burn+ clog accumulate (6 by the lethal turn), shrinking the effective hand exactly when the kill was needed.
3. **Excellent calibration, poor conversion.** Unlike the optimism-biased Run 224, this player's win% labels were honest *and* they diagnosed the fatal error live (idx 357) — but still didn't act 2–3 turns earlier when it mattered. The gap here is execution discipline, not belief.
4. **Sound macro game.** Drafting (Empty Body, Halt, Empty Fist, Pressure Points, Wallop, Foresight), pathing (Lament on Lagavulin, rests timed, Eruption+ smith, free Decay removal), and relic picks were all good. The run was lost on one mid-boss pacing reflex, not on strategy — and the deck's low ceiling (single Wrath entry, no scaling payoff) capped the win% regardless.
