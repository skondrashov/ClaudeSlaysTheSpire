## D16 finding — the discipline was PARTIALLY applied, and the part it skipped is what killed it

**Verdict: D16's check FIRED at the forks — but it priced the WRONG threat, so the funnel-commit regret it was built to prevent was NOT averted. This run does NOT satisfy D16's success criterion (it is one of the two-consecutive-runs-with-no-funnel-commit-regret, and it carries a funnel-commit regret).**

The evidence that the check fired:
- **F13 elite fork (i407):** "F13 ELITE DECISION (priced at the fork)" — children enumerated one floor down, both branches reach Hexaghost, elite branch chosen for the richer reward. Textbook D16.
- **F12 fork (i1959):** at 80 HP, "AVOID a 2nd Act 3 elite (deadly pre-boss). Take Monster(x=1) over Elite(x=2)." Children checked, elite priced as a kill-speed threat, route changed. Correct.
- **F13→F14 fork (i2039), the fatal one:** "PRE-BOSS ROUTE at 37 HP: F13: Unknown(x1)→F14 x2 ELITE (forced - avoid at 37 HP) vs Monster(x2)→F14 x3 SHOP→F15 REST→boss. Take Monster(x2)." The funnel WAS enumerated — the player saw that the Unknown branch funneled into a forced elite and consciously routed AROUND it.

So recitation-vs-application is not the failure this time. The check fired and *changed* the route. The failure is one level deeper: **D16 trains "price the forced ELITE at the fork." It does not train "price the forced MONSTER ROOM at the fork."** The player correctly avoided the elite funnel and routed into a Monster room — which rolled the Transient, a block-check survival fight that is more lethal to a block-light hand than the elite it was avoiding. The player priced the named, expected threat (the elite) and treated the Monster room as the safe default. At 37 HP with a 28-card deck, the Monster room was NOT safe — Act 3 monster rooms include the Transient, Maw, and Reptomancer, any of which is a real kill threat at 37.

This is the precise blind spot D16 doesn't cover and the funnel rule (map.md "check the children of ANY node") technically should: the player checked the children but only re-derived a price for the entity it already feared. **The competing hypothesis in D16 ("only a tool/manifest-level surfacing at the node-choice boundary will bind") gains support here:** the habit fired, enumerated correctly, and still committed a fatal route because the habit's *content* was "avoid the elite," not "price every forced child including monster rooms." A node-choice manifest that surfaced "Act 3 Monster pool includes the Transient — block-check, price your turn-5 block" would have caught this where the habit didn't.

**Recommendation for D16:** the directive should record that run 245 produced a funnel-commit regret DESPITE the check firing — but via an un-priced MONSTER child, not the elite the directive is framed around. That is evidence promoting D10-shape-3 (node-choice surfacing) from problem statement toward a maintainer task, AND it widens the lesson: the priced threat at a fork is not just "forced elite" but "any forced child whose worst-case exceeds current HP." This is a Curate/directive note, not an edit I make here.

## The Transient fight — the central EXECUTION error (loaded page, inverted mechanic)

The Transient page was RECALLED at i2043, immediately before T1. The page says, verbatim: **"Attacking is strictly better than pure blocking"** and **"An attack in the opening hand IS a block card here: a 10-damage attack prevents 10 incoming this turn at the same rate as 10 block."** This is because Shifting (ontology: "Upon losing HP, the enemy loses that much Strength until end of turn") means every point of damage you deal REDUCES that turn's incoming.

The player's T1 read (i2044): **"SHIFTING = gains block when hit, so DON'T attack it (waste)."** This is a flat inversion of the mechanic — the player confused the Transient's Shifting with the Spheric Guardian's "gains block when attacked" (which it had just fought at F37). It then played the entire fight **pure-block, never attacking** — exactly the line the page's "What NOT to Do" section names first: "Play purely defensively (blocking without attacking wastes the Shifting mechanic)."

Consequence: with a block-light hand and Runic Pyramid locking attacks in hand, the player could not reach the block ceiling (T2 took 17, T3 the Fairy fired, T4 the 52 killed it). Had it played its attacks — Heavy Blade (Pen-Nib-doubled), Clothesline (Weak, which the page calls "excellent"), Reaper, Iron Wave — each point of those would have reduced incoming at the same rate as block, AND Clothesline's Weak would have cut the escalating hits another 25%. The player's own deck was full of the exact tools the page lists, and it sat on them citing an inverted rule. (Note: the accidental Str −14 from Distilled Chaos at T3 demonstrated the principle live — reducing the Transient's Strength cratered its damage — yet the player still read attacking as a waste.)

**Diagnosis: this is an EXECUTION error, not a book failure.** The fact was present (transient.md), loaded (RECALL i2043), and named the correct play in bold — and the player did the opposite via a misremembered mechanic. Per the audit heuristic's third case, present + loaded + ignored = execution. No knowledge or awareness fix addresses it; the page already says exactly the right thing in the right place. The fix is a player-discipline note (the recall fired and was misread — a *comprehension* miss, not a retrieval miss).

The low-HP-entry gate added to the page this morning ("do not route in at <~15 HP without an Intangible turn or a guaranteed turn-5 block of 80+") did NOT trigger by its letter — entry was 37 HP, not <15. But the gate's *spirit* — block-light hands cannot cover the 80 — applied. See the recommendation below.
