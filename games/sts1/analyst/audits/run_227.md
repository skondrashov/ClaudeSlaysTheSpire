## The Champ fight, turn by turn (idx 438–473)

Entry: 70/88, potions = Snecko Oil + Duplication, **one empty slot** (empty since Fear Potion at F31; last chance to fill was the F20/F23 shops — 24g/35g on hand). Plan (idx 439): P1 build Str, park at ~220–240, "save Shockwave+ for P2" (matches `heuristics/sts1/bosses/the-champ.md` core rule), mega-turn Duplication + Heavy Blade+ x2 "at Str 10+ that's 120+", "blow through 210 and deep into P2 before Execute cadence." Label 70%.

**Two plan premises were false before the first card was played:**
1. **"Str 10+" was unachievable.** The deck's only Str sources were Inflame (+2) and Spot Weakness (+3) — max +5. Red Skull was acquired AFTER this fight. The 120+ mega-turn never existed.
2. **Anger priced at +6 Str.** The ontology's Anger row says "+6" unqualified; the heuristic's HP-management note says +6 (A0) / **+9 (A4)** / +12 (A19). At A5 the observed P2 numbers — Execute 46 = (10+13)x2, Heavy Slash 31 = 18+13 — give **Str 13, consistent with +9 Anger + 2 Gloats**, not +6. The player's pacing ("P2 Anger (+6 Str...) was undercounted", idx 470) used the ontology's number.

Turns: T1–T9 competent P1 play (Metallicize T1, free-turn damage dumps, Inflame T7, parked at ~225 on T9 — on plan). Then:

- **T10 (idx 456): Shockwave+ played pre-transition** — "for the mega-turn." Direct violation of both the entry's core rule and the player's own T0 plan. The Vuln 5 did boost T11–T12, but the Weak 5 and remaining stacks were purged by Anger after ~2 turns of value, and **nothing was left to blunt P2**, where Weak 5 would have cut Execute 46→34 and Slash 31→23 for three turns.
- **T11 (idx 457–460): mega-turn fired ~70 short of plan.** Duplication on **Bludgeon at Str 2** (~38x2) instead of the planned HB+ x2 at high Str. Champ 225 → ~150: crossed the 210 Anger line with **150 HP left** against a realistic burst of ~30–40/turn — a 4–5-turn P2 against Execute-46 cadence with 42 player HP. "Deep into P2" was never on the table with this turn.
- **T12–T15:** Anger (+Str, purge); Execute 46 took 31 (47→16); next 31-hit took 13 (→3); Snecko Oil spent at T13 digging for block (reasonable, late).
- **T16 (idx 468–469): exact-72 lethal** — 12+36+12+12 vs 10 block + 62 HP, all integers verified. The T14 choice of "set up Vuln and pray" over max-block was **correct and shown correct in the reasoning** (block line dies to the next Execute regardless). Excellent lethal awareness at the bottom of a hole dug upstream.

**Regret #1 (the run's steepest): the T10–T11 crossing decision, ≈ 15–20 win pts.** Best available line: hold Shockwave+ as planned, spend 1–2 more P1 turns (cost ~5–10 HP vs Slash/Taunt chip) to get HB+ in hand with SW/Inflame Str at +5, then cross with Duplication+HB+ (58x2 = 116 + extras → P2 at ~90–100, one Execute at most) **with Shockwave+ available to blunt it**. Forward win% of best line ~65%; of the chosen line at T11 ~45%. Mechanism: plan premises never re-validated against the actual deck (Str ceiling) or the actual A5 Anger value. Classification: **execution error** on the Shockwave+ timing (guidance existed, was loaded — the plan quotes it — and was overridden in the moment); **knowledge gap** on the Anger value (see Book failures).

**Was the 70% entry label a calibration error?** Mostly no — ~5 pts high (my forward: 65%). The deck genuinely had the tools; 80% HP entry; the heuristic was loaded. The label's real defect is that it rested on two false premises (Str 10+, Anger +6) — the belief error was in the *plan parameters*, not the headline number. The player's post-fight self-audit (idx 470: -5, names Anger undercounting, proposes the P2-budget rule) is honest and accurate. No calibration flag; the fix is content (Anger value) + a candidate heuristic (P2 budget).
