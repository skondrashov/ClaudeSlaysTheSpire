## Steepest-regret decisions (ranked)

### 1. Colosseum wave 2 at 47 HP — regret ≈ 8 pts (the real loss)
**FLOOR 31 · WIN% 18% chosen / 26% escape · REGRET ≈ +8%**
After wave 1 the player was at 47/80 (59%). `colosseum.md` was looked up earlier in spirit but the binding rule is: *"Fight only if HP is above 50% after Wave 1 and the deck can handle another multi-enemy fight… Wave 2 rewards are NEVER worth a run-ending death."* 59% clears the 50% floor by a whisker, but the entry also flags **Gremlin Nob in Wave 2** as "devastating" and warns to escape if defensive potions are spent. The player fought, dropped to **19 HP**, and had to spend a Dark Shackles potion to survive a lethal Nob Rush — explicitly reasoning at idx 1483 *"Lizard Tail will catch the lethal Rush and revive."* This is the **same fatal mental model that killed the run two floors later**, surfacing early. The reward (Magic Flower + Shuriken) was good, but it directly produced the 43-HP Champ entry. Escaping wave 2 keeps ~47 HP and a full potion belt into the boss → materially higher win%. **Execution error** (the guidance existed and was even consulted) bleeding into a calibration problem (player did not price "no defensive potions into a 420-HP boss" as the rule demands).

### 2. The fatal final turn — regret ≈ 16 pts within the fight
**FLOOR 33 · WIN% ~6% chosen / ~22% block · REGRET ≈ +16% (conditional on reaching this turn)**
Champ at 168→155 HP, intent **ATTACK = Execute (multi-hit)**, player at **22 HP / 0 block**. The player played Thunderclap (Weak) + Bash+ (offense/Vulnerable) and **ended the turn with zero block**, reasoning (idx 1605): *"Take the 44 Execute → Lizard Tail revives to 40 → ~18 HP."* This is wrong on the mechanic: **Execute is a 2-hit attack (10×2 base) and the Champ had accumulated heavy Strength** — the player fed it ~4 free Gloat/BUFF turns during Phase 1/transition (idx 1584–1598), each +2 Str, on top of Anger's +6. By Execute each hit was ≳ the 40-HP revive value. Lizard Tail absorbs hit 1 (22→0→40), then hit 2 re-kills (40→0). **One-time revive cannot survive a multi-hit attack whose individual hits are lethal.** Correct line: Weak was already applied; spend the turn on **Power Through / Feel No Pain block** (the player had FNP deployed and Power Through in deck — both used elsewhere this very fight) to eat the Execute, *then* burst. Even ~25 block flips the turn from coin-flip-on-a-myth to survivable. **Knowledge gap** (Lizard Tail multi-hit caveat absent) compounded by an **execution error** (chose offense at 0 block at 22 HP — basic lethal-awareness failure).

### 3. Letting the Champ free-Gloat into Phase 2 — regret ≈ 6 pts within the fight
**FLOOR 33 · contributing cause**
Between idx 1584 and 1599 the player chose to chip slowly and "trigger Anger to limit Gloat stacks," but in practice the Champ took multiple BUFF/Gloat turns un-pressured, ballooning its Strength so that Execute outran Lizard Tail's 40-HP revive. `the-champ.md` explicitly says *"Burst through Phase 2 quickly"* and that Execute scales with Phase-1 Gloat stacks. The player read the entry (idx 1534) but executed the opposite of "burst quickly," arriving at the Execute with a half-dead Champ (155 HP) and no defensive margin. **Execution error against loaded guidance.**

1. **The Lizard-Tail-as-shield fallacy, twice.** Idx 1483 (Nob Rush) and idx 1605 (Champ Execute) — the player twice planned to *take* a lethal hit and let Lizard Tail revive, rather than blocking. But the run was lost to (a) one missing fact and (b) two HP-discipline execution errors — not to the dead retrieval system per se. The contextual layer being unreachable is a real defect, but here it was a contributing fog, not the cause.
