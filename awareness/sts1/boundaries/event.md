# Event

Consider, with an event's options on screen:

- The event's own pages, looked up by its name — most named events have an ontology entry (exact outcomes) and many a heuristics entry (the leanings); don't price options from memory of similar events.
- The resource ordering on every trade: HP > deck quality > gold > held potions — [[layer:heuristics, hp-management]]. An option that pays HP for a lower resource starts as a refusal.
- Max-HP costs are dearer than HP costs: they shrink every future heal and the ledger's ceiling for the rest of the run.
- A curse's price includes its removal: a shop within 2-3 floors makes some curse trades profitable; none before the boss makes them dear — [[layer:heuristics, drafting]].
- What "leave" costs — usually nothing. The walk-away option makes the floor a free roll; a trade that can't be priced defaults to it.
- Where this floor sits: the same trade is cheaper after the act's exams than just before them, and near the boss HP-for-reward trades are off the table — [[layer:heuristics, map]].
