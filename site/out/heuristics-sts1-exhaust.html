Exhaust Strategy

  • Before Exhausting, Ask: (1) Can remaining deck survive the enemy's biggest attack? (2) Is the card truly expendable? (3) Am I in first half (thin=good) or second half (thin=fatal)?
  • Random Exhaust Is Run-Ending: Unupgraded True Grit and Havoc both cause random exhaust. Rule: Never play Havoc or unupgraded True Grit when deck contains irreplaceable cards. Upgrade True Grit IMMEDIATELY if taken.
  • Safe Exhaust Targets: Strikes (once better attacks exist), extra Defends, status cards (Slimed, Wound, Burn, Dazed).
  • Never Exhaust: Last copy of best damage card, last high-block card, core combo pieces (Bash when Vuln matters), synergy engines (Fire Breathing when Immolate is in deck).
  • The Long Fight Problem: Exhaust cards front-load power into first 5 turns. After that, deck plays like starter. Against scaling enemies, first 5 turns aren't enough. If 30%+ of deck exhausts/is Ethereal, must win in 5-6 turns.
  • Fiend Fire + Unceasing Top Engine: Exception to long fight problem. After Fiend Fire exhausts hand, Top draws a card. Cycles through draw pile in one mega-turn. With energy from Bloodletting/Offering, deals 50-100+ damage. Strongest combo observed for Ironclad.
  • Boss Exhaust Trap: Mass exhaust (Fiend Fire, Corruption) permanently destroys cards. This is dangerous in two scenarios:
  • Phased bosses (The Champ, Awakened One): Phase transitions cleanse debuffs. Debuffs exhausted by Fiend Fire in Phase 1 are gone forever — cannot be re-applied in Phase 2.
  • Long fights (Time Eater, any 10+ turn fight): Cards exhausted early are gone for the remaining 8-10 turns. Emergency tools (Dark Shackles -15 Str, Intimidate Weak, block cards) that seem redundant on Turn 2 become the difference between life and death when the enemy has +4-6 Str by Turn 10. Before mass-exhausting, separate every card in hand into "truly expendable" (Strikes, status, duplicates) and "needed later" (debuffs, emergency defense, block). Only mass-exhaust when hand contains NO category-2 cards.
