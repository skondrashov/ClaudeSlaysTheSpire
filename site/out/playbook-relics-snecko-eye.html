Snecko Eye (Boss Relic)

EFFECT: Draw 2 additional cards each turn (7 total). Start each combat Confused (all card costs randomized 0-3).

WHY IT'S GOOD: Drawing 7 cards per turn means reliably finding key combo pieces every single turn. The cost randomization averages to 1.5E per card, but high-cost cards (2E+) benefit enormously -- Immolate+ (2E), Heavy Blade (2E), Impervious (2E), Flame Barrier (2E) all have a 25% chance of costing 0E each turn. With 7 cards drawn, the expected number of 0-cost plays is 1-2 per turn. This relic was the single biggest enabler of the first victory.

WHEN TO TAKE: Take when the deck has multiple 2E+ cards. The more expensive the average card cost, the better Snecko Eye becomes. A deck with Immolate+, Heavy Blade, Impervious, Flame Barrier, and Bash+ has 5 cards that can become free. With 7 draws/turn, at least one will usually cost 0.

WHEN TO AVOID: Decks built around 0-cost cards (Anger, Rage) or X-cost cards (Whirlwind) get worse with Snecko Eye. Whirlwind under Confusion costs 0-3 instead of using all remaining energy. 0-cost cards can be randomized to cost 1-3, making them worse.

KEY INTERACTIONS:

  • Headbutt top-decking: Top-decked cards get new Snecko cost rolls when drawn. Headbutt + Immolate+ can guarantee drawing Immolate+ with a new cost roll that might be 0.
  • Dual Wield+: Copies added to hand get Snecko cost rolls. Dual Wield+ on Immolate+ creates two copies, each potentially costing 0-1E.
  • Liquid Memories: Returns a card from discard to hand with a new Snecko cost roll. Retrieving Immolate+ at 0E from Snecko is extremely high value.
  • Blessing of the Forge: Upgrades all hand cards. With 7 cards in hand, this upgrades more cards than normal.
  • Nunchaku: Drawing 7 cards means playing more attacks per turn, triggering Nunchaku's +1E bonus more often.

BOSS FIGHT VALUE: In the Donu and Deca victory, Snecko Eye enabled: Immolate+ at 0E on Turn 1, Heavy Blade at 0E on Turn 5 (killing Donu through block), Spot Weakness+ at 0E on Turn 3, Dual Wield+ at 0E on Turn 4, and Liquid Memories into Immolate+ at 0E on Turn 5. Without these 0-cost rolls, the fight would have taken 10+ turns instead of 6.

KEY INTERACTION -- GAMBLING CHIP: Gambling Chip's mulligan at combat start synergizes strongly with Snecko Eye. When Snecko Eye assigns bad costs (3E) to key cards, Gambling Chip lets you discard them and draw replacements with new cost rolls. This dramatically improves Turn 1 consistency. Present in the second victory.

KEY INTERACTION -- SNECKO OIL: With Snecko Eye already active (Confused), Snecko Oil's cost randomization is redundant -- all cards are already randomized. But the draw 5 effect is pure upside. Using Snecko Oil Turn 1 of a boss fight expands the hand to 10 cards for a massive burst opening. No additional downside since Confusion is already in effect.

KEY INTERACTION -- NECRONOMICON: Snecko Eye randomizes card costs to 0-3. Cards that normally cost 0-1 energy can roll 2+, triggering Necronomicon's doubling effect. This dramatically increases the number of Attacks that benefit from Necronomicon each turn. With 7 cards drawn, the probability of at least one Attack costing 2+ is very high. Barrage (2E base) always qualifies when it rolls 2-3, and Cold Snap (1E base) can trigger when randomized to 2+. The combined effect of Snecko Eye + Necronomicon is one of the strongest damage engines available to the Defect.

DEFECT-SPECIFIC VALUE: Snecko Eye is strong on the Defect because Defect decks often contain a mix of 0-2 cost cards. Drawing 7 cards per turn means more orbs channeled, more Frost passive block, and more chances to play Barrage at reduced cost. The Defect's orb-based defense (passive Frost block) partially compensates for bad cost rolls on block cards because orbs generate block automatically without playing cards.

EVALUATION: The strongest boss relic for Ironclad when the deck has 4+ cards costing 2E. Present in both consecutive Ironclad victories (the only boss relic appearing in both wins). Also powerful on Defect with orb-scaling cards (Barrage, Cold Snap) and Necronomicon synergy. The extra draws solve consistency problems (always find key cards) and the cost randomization provides massive energy efficiency on high rolls. S-tier in Strength-scaling decks with expensive power cards. Take Snecko Eye as Act 2 boss relic whenever the deck has high-cost cards or orb-scaling Attacks.
