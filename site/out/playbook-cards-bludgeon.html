Bludgeon (3E, Attack, 32 damage | +: 42 damage)

The hardest-hitting single Attack in Ironclad's card pool. 32 base damage for 3 energy.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Boss fights and elite fights where a single massive hit matters. With Vulnerable: floor(32 1.5) = 48 damage. With +2 Strength and Vulnerable: floor(34 1.5) = 51 damage. With +4 Strength and Vulnerable: floor(36 * 1.5) = 54 damage. Excellent for triggering Guardian Mode Shift in one hit. Outstanding burst against any high-HP single target.

DECISION POINTS: At 3E, Bludgeon consumes your entire turn. Play it on free turns (enemy defending, buffing, or debuffing) where you would not benefit from blocking. Against The Guardian, save for DEFEND or STRONG_DEBUFF intent turns. Against The Collector, play on the STRONG_DEBUFF turn (Turn 4) for maximum damage while the boss deals no direct damage.

SYNERGIES: Inflame (every point of Strength is amplified by Vulnerable's 1.5x multiplier), Bash+ (3 turns of Vulnerable for repeated Bludgeon value), Double Tap (2x Bludgeon = 96 damage with Vulnerable), Liquid Memories (retrieve from discard for 0 cost).

WEAKNESSES: 3E cost means no block on the turn it is played. Bad against multi-enemy fights where you need to split damage or block multiple sources. Confusion (Snecko) can randomize the cost to 0-3.

UPGRADE: 32 -> 42 damage. Significant but the base card is already strong. Not a top-priority upgrade since the 32 base is sufficient for most purposes.
