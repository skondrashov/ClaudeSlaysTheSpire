Byrd (hallway, Act 2, HP: 25-31 each, appears in groups of 3)

PATTERN (while airborne): 50% Peck (1x5 damage), 30% Caw (+1 Strength), 20% Swoop (12 damage). Cannot use Caw or Swoop twice in a row. Cannot use Peck three times in a row.

WHEN GROUNDED (Flight removed): Stunned -> Headbutt (3 damage) -> Go Airborne (gains 3 Flying) -> returns to normal pattern.

DAMAGE:

  • Peck: 1x5 = 5 damage (each hit is 1, halved by Flight on other Byrds)
  • Swoop: 12 damage
  • Caw: 0 damage, gains 1 Strength
  • Headbutt: 3 damage (only when grounded)
  • Go Airborne: gains 3 Flying

KEY MECHANIC: Flight 3. Takes 50% less attack damage while Flying. Each attack hit removes 1 Flight charge. Resets at start of next turn if not fully removed. Multi-hit and AOE attacks strip Flight faster. Indirect damage (poison, orbs) ignores Flight entirely.

WHAT THIS MEANS: Use Thunderclap to strip 1 Flight from ALL Byrds and apply Vulnerable simultaneously. Focus fire one Byrd at a time -- going from 3 to 2 removes 33% of incoming damage. Once Flight is at 0, burst with Vulnerable attacks to kill. Expect 7-12 turn fights and 20-73 HP lost.

BENCHMARKS: With Thunderclap + burst: 7-turn fight, ~20 HP lost. Without AOE: 8-10 turns, 36+ HP lost. Worst case observed: 8-turn fight with TC+ and Spot Weakness present, 73 HP lost (74 to 1) -- all 3 Byrds attacked on turn 2 for 38 combined damage with only 1 Defend in hand. This fight is the primary Act 2 HP drain and a leading cause of death spirals.

WARNING: If the map forces a Monster room immediately after a Byrd fight with no rest/shop/event option, the run may be lost. 3 Byrds can drain 50-73 HP even with correct play, and Burning Blood (+6) cannot compensate. When planning Act 2 routes, ensure the first 2-3 floors include at least one non-combat room (rest site, shop, event) as a safety valve after potential Byrd encounters.

BRING: AOE (Thunderclap for Flight stripping + Vulnerable -- THE key card), burst damage (Carnage+ nearly one-shots a Byrd), multi-hit attacks (Twin Strike+ strips 2 Flight per play), Metallicize (essential for long fight), Reaper (sustain healing).

AVOID: Small single-hit attacks (halved by Flight and inefficient). Entering below 50% HP. Havoc or unupgraded True Grit (random exhaust can destroy key cards in this long fight).

TURN 2 TRAP: If all 3 Byrds attack simultaneously (after gaining Str from buff turns), combined damage can reach 38+. With only 1 block card in hand, there is no survival path except taking the hit. The deck needs block density (multiple Shrug It Off, Ghostly Armor, Impervious) to survive the worst-case all-attack turns. A 12-card deck with only 5-6 block cards has a significant chance of drawing only 1 block card on any given turn.

THUNDERCLAP IS CRITICAL: Without Thunderclap, Flight 3 on three enemies means 9 hits just to strip all Flight once -- at half damage each. Thunderclap strips 1 Flight from all AND applies Vulnerable for 1E. No other card comes close to this efficiency against Byrds. If Thunderclap is not in the deck by Act 2, the Byrd fight becomes a 36+ HP drain that can end the run.

NOTE: Byrds can appear alongside Chosen (Chosen+Byrd fight). This combines Flight mechanics with Hex (Skills add Dazed). Extremely dangerous -- you need Attacks for Byrd Flight stripping but also need block from Skills, which trigger Hex. Enter at 50%+ HP minimum.
