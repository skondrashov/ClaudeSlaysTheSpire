Fiend Fire (2E, Attack, Exhaust)

Exhausts ALL other cards in hand. Deals 7 damage per card exhausted to a single target.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: When burst will kill the target, when remaining cards are expendable (Strikes, status cards), or on free turns. Turns Slimed/Decay/Dazed/Burn cards into 7 damage each. Critical for curse removal in combat.

DECISION POINTS: After playing Fiend Fire, your hand is EMPTY. Only play when: (1) the burst kills, (2) it's a free turn, or (3) remaining cards are expendable. With 4 other cards: 4x7=28 damage. With Vulnerable: 4x10=40. With +2 Str: 4x13=52.

SYNERGIES:

  • Status/Curse cards (Slimed/Decay/Burn/Dazed become 7+ damage instead of dead draws)
  • Strength (each exhausted card's damage scales: +2 Str = 9/card)
  • Vulnerable (each hit gets 50% bonus)
  • Unceasing Top (CRITICAL COMBO): After Fiend Fire empties hand, Unceasing Top draws a card. If that card is playable and you have energy, play it, hand empties again, Top draws again. Creates a draw engine that can cycle through entire draw pile in one turn.
  • Snecko Oil: Randomize costs before Fiend Fire -- if Fiend Fire costs 0, massive burst with energy left for drawn cards.

MATCHUPS: Slime Boss -- burst + Slimed exhaust. Mystic -- burst through block. Spheric Guardian -- exhaust Artifacts turn then burst. Chosen -- can exhaust Dazed cards while dealing damage, BUT exhausting defensive cards (Uppercut, Defend) leaves you with no block for subsequent turns. If Chosen survives the Fiend Fire turn, the remaining turns have zero block sources. Only use Fiend Fire against Chosen when the burst will kill, or when the exhausted cards are genuinely expendable (Strikes, Dazed, status cards).

CAUTION: Exhausting key cards (Barricade, Clothesline) is permanent. At critically low HP, exhausting everything for survival is correct -- survival > future value. Example: exhausting Barricade to remove a Decay curse at 3 HP is the right call.

GUARDIAN WARNING: Fiend Fire as a primary engine drains the deck unsustainably. Playing Fiend Fire multiple times (turns 3, 5, 7) exhausts 12+ cards by mid-fight, leaving only 8-10 cards in the deck. The Guardian is a 12-14 turn fight requiring 20+ block on 32-damage turns (turns 8-12). With a thin deck, block sources (Defend, Shrug It Off) appear too infrequently. Use Fiend Fire on free turns (DEFEND/STRONG_DEBUFF intents) for burst damage without paying the hand cost. Never plan the deck around Fiend Fire as the sole damage engine -- pair it with immediate-damage cards (Bludgeon, Rampage, Carnage) that don't exhaust the hand.

INDEX SHIFTING DANGER: Fiend Fire is commonly the last card in a multi-card turn plan. If earlier cards shift indices, Fiend Fire may not play. A confirmed death occurred when Iron Wave + Fiend Fire was planned but index shift caused Strike to play instead of Fiend Fire. ALWAYS reference Fiend Fire by name ("play Fiend Fire 0") rather than by index number.
