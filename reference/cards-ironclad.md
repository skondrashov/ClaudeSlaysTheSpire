# Ironclad Cards

Every Ironclad card observed across all runs. Cards are grouped by type.

---

## Starter Cards

### Strike (1E, Attack, 6 damage | +: 9 damage)
Basic attack. Baseline damage card that gets outclassed quickly.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Early floors before better attacks are available. Safe against Gremlin Nob (Attack card).
DECISION POINTS: Is there a better attack to play instead? Could this energy be spent on block or setup?
UPGRADE: 6->9 damage. With Vulnerable: 9->13. Low priority unless mass-upgraded via Ancient Writing event.

### Defend (1E, Skill, 5 block | +: 8 block)
Basic block. Frail reduces this to 3, which is terrible.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: When you need block and have nothing better. Defend+ at 8 block is actually decent.
DECISION POINTS: Against Chosen with Hex active, every Defend adds a Dazed to your draw pile. Against Gremlin Nob, Defend triggers Enrage (+2 Str). Consider whether block is worth the cost.
UPGRADE: 5->8 block (60% increase). Low individual priority but Ancient Writing mass upgrade is excellent.

### Bash (2E, Attack, 8 damage + 2 Vulnerable | +: 10 damage + 3 Vulnerable)
Primary Vulnerable applicator. Opens every fight.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Always. Vulnerable for 2-3 turns means all subsequent attacks deal 50% more damage.
DECISION POINTS: Is this the right turn to apply Vulnerable? On free turns (enemy buffing/debuffing), Bash is ideal. On high-damage incoming turns, 2E on Bash means less block. With Gremlin Nob, Bash is safe (Attack card).
UPGRADE: 8->10 damage, 2->3 turns Vulnerable. The extra Vulnerable turn is enormous: 3 turns of 50% bonus vs 2 turns. Top upgrade priority in any deck.
SYNERGIES: Headbutt (put Bash+ back on top to refresh Vulnerable), Dropkick (free cycling when target is Vulnerable), all Attack cards (50% more damage).
MATCHUPS: Lagavulin -- refresh Vulnerable after it wears off by using Headbutt to top-deck Bash+. Guardian -- apply on free turns (DEFEND/STRONG_DEBUFF intent). Gremlin Nob -- safe to play (Attack card).

## Attack Cards

### Headbutt (1E, Attack, 9 damage | +: 12 damage)
Put a card from your discard pile on top of your draw pile.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: When you have a high-value card in discard you need next turn. Strongest with predictable enemy patterns and specific targets (Bash+, Feed, Impervious).

DECISION POINTS: Before playing, check your discard pile. What do you need next turn given enemy intent? Is there a key card -- Bash+ to refresh Vulnerable, Impervious for a big incoming hit, Feed for a low-HP kill -- that beats a random draw?

UPGRADE: 9->12 damage. With Vulnerable: 13->18. High priority if Headbutt is a regular play. In one fight, unupgraded Headbutt (9 damage) left Mystic at 1 HP, extending the fight 2+ turns. Headbutt+ would have killed.

SYNERGIES: Carnage+ (guarantee 28-42 next turn), Feed (guarantee kill shot), Bash+ (refresh Vulnerable on demand), small discard pile (more control).

MATCHUPS: Lagavulin -- top Bash+ to refresh Vulnerable after it wears off. With Lagavulin's -1 Str debuff: floor((12-1)*1.5) = 16 Vuln damage. Guardian -- guarantee burst damage draw for Mode Shift phases. Centurion+Mystic -- guarantee Feed or burst for Mystic kill.

### Pommel Strike (1E, Attack, 9 damage, draw 1 | +: 10 damage, draw 2)
Damage + draw. Replaces itself in hand while dealing more than Strike.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Always. The draw is the real value -- it cycles through the deck to find key cards.
DECISION POINTS: Play early in the turn to draw into more options. Against Byrds, the 9 damage is halved by Flight but the draw still finds better answers.
UPGRADE: 9->10 damage, draw 1->draw 2. The double draw makes this one of the best cycling cards in the game. High priority upgrade.
SYNERGIES: Any deck that wants to find specific cards (Bash+, Fiend Fire, Feed). Snecko Oil (draw into 0-cost cards).

### Hemokinesis (1E, Attack, 15 damage, costs 2 HP)
High burst for 1 energy. The 2 HP self-damage is minor when healthy.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Opening turns, boss burst damage, finishing blows where HP cost is irrelevant.
DECISION POINTS: Check current HP. At low HP in a long fight, the 2 HP per play adds up. With Vulnerable on target: floor(15*1.5) = 22 damage for 1E -- exceptional burst.
SYNERGIES: Vulnerable (22 damage for 1E), kill shots where HP cost doesn't matter.

### Clothesline (2E, Attack, 12 damage + 2 Weak)
Expensive but applies Weak. Reduces enemy attack damage by 25% for 2 turns.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Against single big attackers where Weak saves more HP than alternative plays. Against Hexaghost when no other Weak source is available.
DECISION POINTS: Is 2E for 12 damage + Weak worth more than two 1E cards? Against multiple enemies, you can only Weak one. Consider energy economy.
UPGRADE: Values unconfirmed.
MATCHUPS: Hexaghost -- Weak reduces 7x6=42 Inferno to 7x4=28. This is a lifesaving application.

### Iron Wave (1E, Attack, 5 damage + 5 block | +: 7 damage + 7 block)
Dual-purpose card. Does two things at once for 1 energy.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: When you need both offense and defense but only have 1 energy to spare. Against Chosen with Hex (Attack card, no Dazed penalty). During Guardian Sharp Hide (block absorbs retaliation, then deal damage).
DECISION POINTS: Is splitting between 5 damage and 5 block better than fully committing to one? Usually yes when you need both. With Strength, only the damage scales (5+Str). Block is affected by Frail and Dexterity, not Strength.
UPGRADE: 5/5->7/7. Both halves improve 40%. High upgrade priority -- one of the best multi-purpose cards available.
SYNERGIES: Metallicize (passive block + Iron Wave block = solid defense per turn), Strength (damage half scales).

### Carnage (2E, Attack, 20 damage, Ethereal | +: 28 damage, Ethereal)
Massive single-target burst. Must be played or it exhausts permanently.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Boss fights for big burst turns. Against Guardian Mode Shift (fast phase transitions). Any fight where 2E for 28+ damage is game-changing.
DECISION POINTS: Can you afford 2E on damage this turn, or do you need block? If drawn on a must-block turn, you either spend 2E on damage or lose Carnage permanently. This tension is manageable with enough block cards.
UPGRADE: 20->28 damage. With Vulnerable: 30->42. One of the highest single-card damage outputs. Top priority upgrade.
SYNERGIES: Headbutt (guarantee Carnage+ on top of draw pile for next turn -- used repeatedly for consistent 28-42 damage turns), Vulnerable (42 damage).
MATCHUPS: Guardian -- 42 Vuln damage triggers Mode Shift fast. Byrds -- 28 damage halved to ~14 nearly one-shots a Byrd.

### Uppercut (2E, Attack, 13 damage + 1 Weak + 1 Vulnerable)
Applies BOTH major debuffs in one card.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Against single targets where both debuffs matter. Against Artifact enemies (strips 2 charges).
DECISION POINTS: At 2E, is dual-debuff worth the cost? Against Spheric Guardian (3 Artifact), Uppercut strips 2 charges efficiently. Against Gremlin Nob, it's safe (Attack card).
UPGRADE: Likely extends debuff duration to 2 turns each.
SYNERGIES: Artifact stripping (each debuff consumes 1 charge separately).
MATCHUPS: Spheric Guardian -- strips 2 of 3 Artifact charges in one play.

### Searing Blow (1E, Attack, 12 damage)
Decent damage for 1 energy. Can theoretically be upgraded multiple times (unique mechanic).

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Filler attack when better options aren't available. With Vulnerable: floor(12*1.5) = 18 damage.
DECISION POINTS: Straightforward -- play when you need damage.

### Twin Strike (1E, Attack, likely 5+5 = 10 total)
Multi-hit attack. Each hit applies separately.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Against Flight enemies (strips 2 Flight). For Pen Nib counting (each hit counts toward the 10-hit counter).
DECISION POINTS: Against Flight enemies, multi-hit is more valuable than single-hit for the same total damage.

### Cleave (1E, Attack, ~8 AOE damage)
Cheap AOE damage to all enemies. Does NOT apply Vulnerable.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Multi-enemy fights for raw AOE damage. Good on free turns (turn 1 of Cultist fights) to soften all targets.
DECISION POINTS: Cleave vs Thunderclap -- Thunderclap applies Vulnerable but deals less damage. Usually Thunderclap is better for the Vulnerable.

### Whirlwind (XE, Attack, 5 damage X times to ALL enemies)
AOE that scales with energy. Uses ALL remaining energy.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Turn 1 with bonus energy (Lantern, Seeing Red+) for massive AOE. Against Slime Boss split (hits both slimes). Against Sentries (45 total AOE for 3E).
DECISION POINTS: Play LAST -- it uses all remaining energy. Plan other plays first, then dump remaining energy into Whirlwind. With Strength, each hit gains +Str damage.
UPGRADE: 5->8 per hit. Significant improvement.
SYNERGIES: Extra energy (Lantern, Seeing Red+, Berserk), Strength (each hit gains bonus).
MATCHUPS: Slime Boss split -- hits both slimes. Sentries -- 15 damage to all 3. Gremlin Leader -- clears summoned gremlins.

### Thunderclap (1E, Attack, 4 AOE + 1 Vulnerable ALL | +: 7 AOE + 1 Vulnerable ALL)
Cheap mass Vulnerable. The best setup card for multi-enemy fights.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Always in multi-enemy fights. Turn 1 opener to set up 50% more damage on everything. Strips 1 Flight per Byrd hit and 1 Artifact per enemy.
DECISION POINTS: Against single enemies, worse than Bash+ (1 turn Vulnerable vs 3 turns). Against multiple enemies, one of the best cards in the game.
UPGRADE: 4->7 AOE damage. With Vulnerable on targets: ~6->10 damage each. Significant AOE improvement. Worth upgrading when it's a regular play.
SYNERGIES: Dropkick (sets up free cycling on all Vulnerable targets), all Attack cards (50% more damage to all enemies).
MATCHUPS: Byrds -- strips 1 Flight from each + applies Vulnerable. Sentries -- strips 1 Artifact from each. Slime Boss split -- mass Vulnerable on both slimes.

### Fiend Fire (2E, Attack, Exhaust)
Exhausts ALL other cards in hand. Deals 7 damage per card exhausted to a single target.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: When burst will kill the target, when remaining cards are expendable (Strikes, status cards), or on free turns. Turns Slimed cards into 7 damage each.
DECISION POINTS: After playing Fiend Fire, your hand is EMPTY. Only play when: (1) the burst kills, (2) it's a free turn, or (3) remaining cards are expendable. With 4 other cards: 4x7=28 damage. With Vulnerable: 4x10=40. With +2 Str: 4x13=52.
SYNERGIES: Status cards (Slimed becomes 7 damage instead of dead draw), Strength (each exhausted card's damage scales), Vulnerable (each hit gets 50% bonus).
MATCHUPS: Slime Boss -- burst + Slimed exhaust. Mystic -- burst through block.

### Feed (1E, Attack, 10 damage, Exhaust, +3 Max HP on kill)
One of the strongest Ironclad cards. Permanent Max HP scaling.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Whenever you can land the killing blow on an enemy. Each kill adds 3 Max HP permanently. Over a full run, Feed accumulates 12-18+ Max HP -- more than any single relic.
DECISION POINTS: Is the enemy low enough for Feed to kill? With Vulnerable: floor(10*1.5) = 15 damage. Plan other attacks to soften the target, then Feed for the kill. In multi-enemy fights, soften one target with other attacks then Feed kill it.
UPGRADE: Unknown exact values.
SYNERGIES: Headbutt (place Feed on top when an enemy is low HP -- guarantee the kill shot), Vulnerable (15 damage kill threshold).
MATCHUPS: Best targets are low-HP enemies: Byrds, Mystic, Gremlins, Cultists. Feed strategy: take it early for maximum kills over the run.

### Reaper (2E, Attack, deals damage to ALL enemies, heals HP equal to unblocked damage)
Essential sustain card. Damage + healing in one.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Multi-enemy fights where you heal from hitting all enemies. With Strength, both damage and healing scale.
DECISION POINTS: Reaper heals based on UNBLOCKED damage -- enemies with block reduce healing. In scaling fights (Cultists, Gremlin Leader), Shockwave+ on the first attack turn is higher priority than Reaper healing. Prevention > healing.
SYNERGIES: Inflame (+2 Str boosts damage to all enemies = more healing), Vulnerable (bonus damage = bonus healing), multi-enemy fights (heal from sum of all damage dealt).

### Dropkick (1E, Attack, 5 damage | +: 8 damage)
If target is Vulnerable: gain 1 energy + draw 1 card. Effectively FREE when target is Vulnerable.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: With reliable Vulnerable application (Bash+, Thunderclap). Infinite cycling tool -- costs 1E, refunds 1E, deals damage, draws a card.
DECISION POINTS: Is the target Vulnerable? If yes, always play -- it's free damage + cycling. If no, it's a terrible card (5/8 damage, no benefit). Do NOT play against non-Vulnerable targets.
UPGRADE: 5->8 damage. With Vulnerable: 7->12 damage. The upgrade matters because the free damage is significantly higher.
SYNERGIES: Bash+ (3 turns Vulnerable = 3 free Dropkick plays), Thunderclap (mass Vulnerable = Dropkick any target).
MATCHUPS: Book of Stabbing -- Dropkick+ dealt 12 Vuln damage three times as free actions = 36 total damage for 0 net energy.

## Skill Cards

### Shrug It Off (1E, Skill, 8 block + draw 1 | +: 11 block + draw 1)
Block + draw. Replaces itself while providing defense.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Always. The draw means you get block without losing card advantage.
DECISION POINTS: Play early in the turn when possible -- the drawn card may change your plan. Against Gremlin Nob, do NOT play (Skill triggers Enrage).
UPGRADE: 8->11 block. Significant improvement. Medium-high priority.

### True Grit (1E, Skill, 7 block + exhaust RANDOM card | +: 9 block + exhaust CHOSEN card)
Upgraded version lets you CHOOSE which card to exhaust. Unupgraded version exhausts RANDOMLY.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: True Grit+ is excellent -- block + controlled deck thinning. Exhaust status cards (Slimed, Wound, Burn) while gaining block.
DECISION POINTS: CRITICAL -- is this upgraded? Unupgraded True Grit is a ticking time bomb. In a thin deck, random exhaust may destroy your best card. True Grit+ lets you choose expendable cards (Strikes, status cards). Before playing unupgraded, check: are there cards in hand you absolutely cannot lose?
UPGRADE: 7->9 block, random->chosen exhaust. THE most important upgrade in the game. Random exhaust destroyed Whirlwind, Bash, and Pommel Strike in one fight, directly causing death. True Grit+ with chosen exhaust won the same boss fight in a different run. Upgrade priority: #1 whenever True Grit is in the deck.
SYNERGIES: Dark Embrace (draw on exhaust), Charon's Ashes (3 AOE on exhaust). But be careful of over-exhausting -- keep enough cards to survive the enemy's biggest attack.
MATCHUPS: Gremlin Nob -- do NOT play (Skill triggers Enrage).

### Shockwave (2E, Skill, applies Weak + Vulnerable to ALL enemies, Exhaust | +: 3 turns each)
Mass debuff. One of the most powerful cards in the game. Exhausts after use — but returns to your deck next fight. Play it every fight you draw it.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Multi-enemy fights and boss fights. Mass Weak reduces all incoming damage by 25%. Mass Vulnerable boosts all attacks by 50%.
DECISION POINTS: TIMING IS CRITICAL. Play on the FIRST ATTACK TURN of multi-enemy fights. For 3 Cultists, that's turn 2 (turn 1 is free). Never save for turn 4+ -- by then enemies have already dealt massive unmitigated damage. Preventing damage with early Weak far exceeds healing from Reaper. Priority: Shockwave+ on first attack turn > Reaper > single-target damage. NEVER hold Shockwave "for a harder fight" -- exhaust resets between fights, so you'll have it again next fight regardless.
UPGRADE: 2->3 turns of each debuff. Massive improvement -- 50% more duration. High priority.
SYNERGIES: Multi-enemy fights (debuffs apply to ALL), Dropkick (Vulnerable on all targets = free cycling on any of them).
MATCHUPS: Hexaghost -- Weak reduces 7x6=42 Inferno to 7x4=28. MANDATORY for Hexaghost survival. Gremlin Leader -- mass debuff on Leader + all gremlins. 3 Cultists -- play turn 2, not turn 4. Slime Boss split -- mass debuff on both slimes.

### Impervious (2E, Skill, 30 block, Exhaust)
Best single-card block in the game. 30 block for 2E.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Against the biggest attack of the fight. Guardian's 32-damage attack (take only 2). Hexaghost Inferno.
DECISION POINTS: Save for the biggest incoming hit. Using 30 block on a 16-damage turn is wasteful -- Defends handle that. One-time use means timing matters. Against Gremlin Nob, do NOT play (Skill triggers Enrage).
MATCHUPS: Guardian -- absorbs the 32-damage attack (take only 2). Gremlin Leader -- turn 1 opener for 30 block safety.

### Burning Pact (1E, Skill, Exhaust 1 chosen card from hand, draw 2)
Controlled exhaust + card draw. Net +1 card in hand.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Removing status cards (Slimed, Dazed, Wound). Cycling to find key cards. Thinning in-combat deck safely.
DECISION POINTS: What's expendable in hand? Exhaust status cards or extra Strikes. Against Gremlin Nob and Chosen, do NOT play (Skill card triggers Enrage/Hex).

### Disarm (1E, Skill, reduce enemy Strength by 2 permanently, Exhaust)
Permanent Strength reduction. Extremely powerful against scaling enemies.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Against enemies that gain Strength over time (Gremlin Leader, Cultist). Unlike Weak (temporary 25% reduction), Disarm permanently removes 2 damage from every attack.
DECISION POINTS: Save for the enemy with the most dangerous Strength scaling. Against multi-hit enemies, the value compounds (5-hit attack loses 10 total damage). Against Gremlin Nob and Chosen, do NOT play (Skill triggers Enrage/Hex).
MATCHUPS: Gremlin Leader -- reduces Rally effectiveness. Against multi-hit enemies.

### Intimidate (0E, Skill, apply 1 Weak to ALL enemies, Exhaust)
Free mass Weak. Zero downside.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: ALWAYS play when drawn. 0 energy cost means it never competes with other cards. Reduces all enemy attack damage by 25% for 1 turn.
DECISION POINTS: Play immediately on draw. There is zero reason to hold this card. Against Gremlin Nob and Chosen, do NOT play (Skill triggers Enrage/Hex).
SYNERGIES: Letter Opener (every Skill played deals 5 damage -- Intimidate is free 5 damage + mass Weak).
MATCHUPS: Guardian -- Weak reduces 32-damage attack to 24. Multi-enemy fights -- affects all enemies for free.

### Ghostly Armor (1E, Skill, 10 block, Ethereal)
Strong block for 1 energy (double Defend). Ethereal means must play or lose permanently.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Whenever it's in hand. Always play -- losing it permanently is worse than wasting block.
DECISION POINTS: Do you have 1E to spare? If yes, always play. If forced to choose between Ghostly Armor and a critical play, remember: losing Ghostly Armor permanently is a real cost.

### Power Through (1E, Skill, 15 block, adds 2 Wounds to hand)
Massive block (triple Defend) but pollutes hand with 2 unplayable Wounds.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Must-survive turns with massive incoming damage. When you have exhaust tools (True Grit+, Fiend Fire) to remove the Wounds afterward.
DECISION POINTS: Is 15 block worth 2 dead draws in future hands? In long fights without exhaust tools, the Wounds accumulate and ruin draws. In short fights or with exhaust, the 15 block is worth it.

### Seeing Red+ (0E, Skill, gain 2 Energy, Exhaust)
Free energy. Costs 0 to play, gains 2E, exhausts.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Any turn where you have cards to play but not enough energy. Effectively adds 2E to your turn for free.
DECISION POINTS: Play early in the turn so you can use the 2E on remaining cards. Excellent for enabling expensive plays (Bash+ 2E + another 2E card in the same turn).
SYNERGIES: Expensive cards (Carnage 2E, Shockwave 2E, Fiend Fire 2E). Enables playing them alongside other cards.

## Power Cards

### Inflame (1E, Power, gain 2 Strength permanently)
Cheap, permanent damage scaling. Every Attack deals 2 more damage for the entire fight.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Play as early as possible in long fights (bosses, elites) for maximum value. At 1E cost, extremely efficient.
DECISION POINTS: Is this turn a setup turn or a survival turn? On free turns (enemy buffing/debuffing), always play Inflame. Damage formula with Inflame + Weak: floor((base+2)*0.75). Powers do NOT trigger Gremlin Nob Enrage.
SYNERGIES: Reaper (Strength increases damage to ALL enemies = more healing), Whirlwind (each hit gains +2 damage), every Attack card.
MATCHUPS: Guardian Defensive Mode -- Inflame is a Power, not an Attack, so no Sharp Hide retaliation.

### Metallicize (1E, Power, gain 3 block at end of each turn permanently)
Passive block every turn. The block equivalent of Strength.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Play as early as possible in long fights. Over 10 turns: 30 free block. Two copies provide 6 block/turn -- a game-changing defensive setup that handles many attack patterns with just 1 Defend card.
DECISION POINTS: Play on the first free turn available. The earlier it's played, the more total block it provides. Powers do NOT trigger Gremlin Nob Enrage.
SYNERGIES: Iron Wave (passive block + Iron Wave block = solid defense), second Metallicize (6 block/turn is exceptional).
MATCHUPS: Guardian -- 6 block/turn (with 2 copies) handles many attack patterns (8x2=16, 9 damage) with only 1 Defend needed. Hexaghost -- critical for the 13-turn fight. Byrds -- essential for the long attrition fight.

### Dark Embrace (2E, Power, whenever a card is exhausted, draw 1)
Draw engine for exhaust-heavy decks.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: With multiple exhaust sources (True Grit+, status cards, Ethereal cards). Every exhaust replaces itself in hand.
DECISION POINTS: At 2E, it's expensive to set up. Play on free turns. Powers do NOT trigger Gremlin Nob Enrage. WARNING: encourages over-exhausting, which can thin the deck dangerously.
SYNERGIES: True Grit+ (exhaust + draw every turn), Charon's Ashes (exhaust = draw + 3 AOE damage), status cards (exhausting Slimed/Dazed draws a real card).

### Berserk (0E, Power, gain 2 Vulnerable on YOURSELF, gain 1 energy per turn)
DANGEROUS. Self-Vulnerable (take 50% more damage) for 2 turns. Permanent +1 energy.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: ONLY when enemies are not attacking for the next 2 turns AND you can survive the Vulnerable penalty afterward. In practice, almost never safe.
DECISION POINTS: Can you survive 2 turns of taking 50% more damage from ALL enemies? Against multiple enemies, the combined boosted damage is often lethal. Against bosses at low HP, the self-Vulnerable is suicidal. The energy gain doesn't matter if you die.
MATCHUPS: Hexaghost -- NEVER play. Burns + self-damage + Vulnerable is a death spiral. Multi-enemy fights -- NEVER play. Combined 50% boosted damage from all enemies is overwhelming.

### Brutality (0E, Power, lose 1 HP/turn, draw 1 extra card/turn)
DANGEROUS in long fights. Extra draw at the cost of permanent HP drain.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Short fights (3-4 turns) where the extra draw finds lethal faster. With healing (Reaper, Feed) to offset self-damage.
DECISION POINTS: How long will this fight last? In a 9-turn fight, Brutality costs 9 HP minimum. Against Hexaghost with Burns (2-4 HP/turn), Brutality compounds the self-damage to 3-5 HP/turn total. NEVER play against bosses with status card damage (Hexaghost Burns) or fights expected to last 7+ turns.
MATCHUPS: Hexaghost -- NEVER play. Burns + Brutality = death spiral (20-30 HP from self-damage alone).
