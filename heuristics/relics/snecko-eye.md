# Snecko Eye

Drawing 7 cards per turn means reliably finding key combo pieces every turn. Cost randomization averages 1.5E/card, but high-cost cards (2E+) benefit enormously -- 25% chance of costing 0E each turn. Present in both consecutive [[characters/Ironclad]] victories.

WHEN TO TAKE: Deck has multiple 2E+ cards. The more expensive the average card cost, the better. A deck with [[cards/Immolate]], [[cards/Heavy Blade]], [[cards/Impervious]], [[cards/Flame Barrier]], [[cards/Bash]] has 5 cards that can become free.

WHEN TO AVOID: Decks built around 0-cost cards ([[cards/Anger]], Rage) or X-cost cards ([[cards/Whirlwind]]) get worse. 0-cost cards randomized to 1-3 makes them worse.

KEY SYNERGIES:
- **[[relics/Gambling Chip]]**: Mulligan bad cost rolls for new ones. Dramatically improves Turn 1.
- **[[relics/Necronomicon]]**: Cards randomized to 2+ trigger doubling. Dramatically increases trigger frequency.
- **[[cards/Headbutt]]**: Top-decked cards get new cost rolls.
- **[[relics/Nunchaku]]**: 7 cards drawn = more Attacks played per turn.

S-tier for [[buffs/Strength]]-scaling decks with expensive power cards. The extra draws solve consistency problems and cost randomization provides massive energy efficiency. Take as [[acts/Act 2]] boss relic whenever deck has high-cost cards.

DEFECT VALUE: Strong because [[characters/Defect]]'s orb-based defense partially compensates for bad cost rolls on block cards (orbs generate block automatically without playing cards).
