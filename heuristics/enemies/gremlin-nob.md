# Gremlin Nob

**Core rule:** Play ZERO Skills. No Defend, no True Grit, no Shrug It Off, no Shockwave, no Impervious. Only Attacks and Powers. Use potions for block instead of Skill cards.

**Turn 1 is free.** Nob always Bellows. Go all-in on damage. Skills played Turn 1 (before Bellow) are safe because Enrage is not yet active.

**Benchmark:** 3-4 turn kill with zero Skills. Strength Potion on free Turn 1 maximizes value. Playing 3 Skills gives Nob +6 Str, turning a manageable fight into near-death.

## Character Matchups

**Ironclad:** Starter deck is balanced (5 Attacks including Bash vs 5 Defends). Manageable if you only play Attacks. Bash is safe (Attack card).

**Silent:** Worst matchup among base characters. Starter deck has 9+ Skills vs 5 Attacks. Even "careful" play forces 5-7 Skill triggers across a 7-turn fight because pure-Attack hands are rare. Blade Dance generates Shiv Attacks but is itself a Skill that triggers Enrage. When Hexaghost is the boss, do NOT waste Piercing Wail on Nob — save it for Inferno.

**Defect:** Worst elite matchup. 6 Skills (4 Defend + Zap + Dualcast) vs 4 Attacks. Both Zap and Dualcast are Skills that trigger Enrage. Do NOT path into Nob without picking up multiple Attack cards first (Compile Driver, Sweeping Beam, Ball Lightning, Cold Snap). Cold Snap is the best Nob card — Attack that also channels Frost for passive block (orb passives don't trigger Enrage). Turn 1 is the only safe turn for Skills.

## Deck Composition Check

Before pathing into a potential Nob fight, count Attacks vs Skills. If Skills outnumber Attacks (7+ Skills vs 4 Attacks), most hands contain 3-4 Skills and only 1-2 Attacks. Even with perfect discipline, the kill takes 7+ turns. A Skill-heavy deck makes Nob near-unwinnable.
