# Donu and Deca

**Core rule:** AOE is king (every point does double duty). Focus fire Donu first (stops [[buffs/Strength]] scaling). Strength scaling + [[cards/Heavy Blade]] for the finisher.

## The Strength-Ramp Clock

Donu's Circle of Power gives BOTH bosses +3 Strength every other turn — the block requirement grows ~+6 per cycle for as long as Donu lives. At A4+ the beams start at 12x2 = 24 and climb past 40 within ~6 turns. This makes the fight a race with a deadline, not an attrition check: "block density for 6-10 turns" means ESCALATING density, and a deck whose block ceiling is flat (~20-25/turn) gets outrun by the clock no matter how well it plays. At the pre-boss commitment node, price BOTH sides: your sustained damage/turn against ~500 combined HP, AND your block ceiling against the beam escalation curve.

**Kill order tradeoff (stated so you don't re-derive it mid-fight):** focusing Donu costs you Deca's Square of Protection (16 block to both per cycle, slowing your kill) and Deca's Dazed clog while Donu dies. That cost is real — but it is bounded and constant, while Donu's Strength ramp compounds and raises the LETHAL bar every cycle. Kill Donu first even without AOE, and answer the Square cycles with burst timing rather than switching targets.

**[[relics/Bag of Marbles]] anti-synergy:** the combat-start Vulnerable is eaten by each boss's [[buffs/Artifact]] 2 — it strips a charge from each but lands nothing. Plan debuffs accordingly (two strips per boss before anything sticks).

## Winning Strategy

1. **AOE is king.** [[cards/Immolate]] hits BOTH bosses every cast — multiple copies in one turn (via Dual Wield or cheap [[relics/Snecko Eye]] costs) produce 100+ total AOE.
2. **Focus fire Donu.** All single-target attacks go to Donu. AOE handles Deca passively.
3. **Strength scaling is the finisher.** [[cards/Spot Weakness]] -> [[cards/Limit Break]] -> Heavy Blade (50+ damage) as lethal finisher.
4. **[[relics/Snecko Eye]] is exceptional.** 7 cards/turn with random costs. Multiple 0-cost Immolate+ plays win the fight.
5. **[[cards/Dual Wield]] on Immolate+** creates three copies in hand for 100+ AOE.

## Preparation Checklist

1. AOE damage (Immolate+ is the best card)
2. Strength scaling (Spot Weakness+, [[cards/Inflame]], Limit Break, Heavy Blade)
3. HP for an expected 30-50 HP fight (combined incoming is 20-32/turn over 6-10 turns) — run the ledger before the pre-boss rest site decision
4. Block density for 6-10 turns — ESCALATING (see the Strength-Ramp Clock above): the late-fight turns need 1.5-2x the early-fight block
5. Potions: [[potions/Snecko Oil]] Turn 1, [[potions/Duplication Potion]] on 0-cost Immolate+, [[potions/Strength Potion]] compounds with Limit Break

## Key Cards

- **Immolate+:** Best card in the fight (28-34 AOE per play)
- **Heavy Blade+:** 5x Str multiplier finisher
- **Dual Wield+:** Copies Immolate+ for triple AOE turns
- **Limit Break+ (no exhaust):** Most impactful upgrade for this fight -- enables repeated Str doubling
- **Flex+ with Limit Break+:** 4 temporary Str doubled by Limit Break+ = effectively 10 permanent Str
- **[[cards/Evolve]]:** Converts Burns from Immolate into card draws

## Silent / No-AOE Decks

The Winning Strategy above is Ironclad-shaped (Immolate, Heavy Blade). Without AOE the fight is harder, not different: the kill-order rule and the clock still govern.

- A Shiv or poison engine that one-shots hallway enemies is NOT automatically ready for this fight — the check here is block-per-turn against the escalating beams, and a saturated damage engine with a flat ~20-block ceiling loses on the clock. Before committing, the marginal pick/upgrade belongs to defensive scaling ([[cards/Footwork]], [[cards/After Image]]-class effects), not a fourth damage multiplier.
- [[debuffs/Weak]] on the beams is the cheapest way to bend the escalation curve — bring a repeatable Weak source ([[cards/Leg Sweep]], [[cards/Neutralize]]) and keep it for beam turns.
- Poison is single-target: it does not get AOE's double duty, so the Donu-first rule costs less to follow — stack poison on Donu and let block answer Deca.

## Potion Timing

- [[enemies/Snecko]] Oil Turn 1 (expands hand to 10 cards)
- Strength Potion Turn 2 (compounds with every attack)
- Duplication Potion on 0-cost Immolate+ (wait for favorable Snecko roll)
- [[potions/Liquid Memories]] retrieves Immolate+ from discard at new Snecko cost
