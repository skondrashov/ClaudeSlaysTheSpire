# Void

**Status card.** Unplayable. Ethereal. When drawn, lose 1 Energy.

**Source:** Added to draw pile by Sludge (from [[bosses/Awakened One]] and some Act 3 enemies).

## Why Void Matters

Void drains 1E on the turn it is drawn. In fights where every point of energy determines whether you can fully block, losing 1E can be fatal. Confirmed fatal: Run 221 died by exactly 1 HP against Awakened One Phase 2 because Void was drawn on the lethal turn, reducing energy from 4 to 3. With 4E the player could block 25 (surviving at 4 HP). With 3E they could only block 20 (dying at -1 HP).

## Management

- Void is Ethereal: if not played, it exhausts at end of turn. It does NOT persist in hand.
- With [[relics/Runic Pyramid]]: Void stays in hand but does NOT drain energy again on subsequent turns (only on draw). However, it occupies a hand slot.
- Void cannot be played, so it cannot be removed via normal card play. Exhaust tools ([[cards/Burning Pact]], [[cards/True Grit]], [[cards/Second Wind]]) can remove it from the deck permanently.
- After Sludge adds Void to the deck, count the number of Voids and plan for potential energy loss on critical survival turns.

## Key Interactions

- **Runic Pyramid:** Void drawn once drains 1E, then sits in hand harmlessly (no repeated drain). But it occupies a slot, reducing effective hand size.
- **Draw effects:** Cards that draw ([[cards/Shrug It Off]], [[cards/Pommel Strike]], [[cards/Battle Trance]]) can pull Void from the draw pile, triggering the energy drain mid-turn. Be aware of this when planning draw-heavy turns after Sludge.
- **Corruption+FNP engine:** Void is not a Skill, so Corruption does not make it free or exhaust it. Must be removed via dedicated exhaust tools.
