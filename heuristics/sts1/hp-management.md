# HP Management

- **HP is an act-level resource, not per-fight.** "I can afford 8 damage" thinking is wrong. The goal in EVERY hallway fight is zero damage taken. The pathing arithmetic below never licenses paying a price — minimization is unconditional; pathing only decides which fights you take.
- **Resource ordering: HP > deck quality > gold > held potions.** Every fight is minmaxed for damage taken, not for resources conserved. Spending an expiring resource (a potion, a once-per-fight effect) to protect HP is the default trade, not a desperation move — see Potion Economy in [[layer:heuristics, combat]]. The greedy pattern (get through the fight cheaply, save everything) reads as efficient and loses runs: saved potions expire unused while chip damage compounds into a death two fights later.
- **Pathing is a ledger you price LIVE, against this deck.** There is no stored cost for a fight — what a fight costs depends on what you bring, and the spread is huge (the same elite has cost one deck triple what it cost another). The enemy and boss pages state the fight's **damage clock** (what it deals per turn, how it scales, what shape the damage takes) and its **reduction levers** (kill order, debuffs, burst windows); you price the fight at the routing node: your turns-to-kill against its HP, times its per-turn clock over those turns, minus what your mitigation shape actually blocks. The pathing question is then arithmetic: current HP, minus your derived price for every forced fight between here and the next recovery, must stay above the **largest single-turn spike** on the path (a mechanical number, from the attack tables) — with margin for a bad draw. If the subtraction goes negative, change the path or recover first.
- **Why not entry thresholds:** a rule like "enter above 60%" is a cached version of that subtraction that throws away your deck, the rest of the path, and your recovery options. The cache misleads in both directions — it forbids cheap fights a burst deck wins cleanly at 45%, and it blesses expensive fights a slow deck shouldn't take at 75%. Worse, it teaches that HP only matters at checkpoints. Do the subtraction; the fight's damage structure is the knowledge worth keeping.
- **Why not stored prices either:** a remembered "this fight costs 30-50" anchors you toward paying it and stops the cheaper-line search — the question in every fight is what line minimizes the loss, and the question at every audit is whether a cheaper line existed. Historical paid-costs are analyst data (analyst/fight-costs.md), keyed to the deck that paid them — they inform pattern-finding, never budgets.
- **Kill speed is the dominant price term.** Scaling fights ([[encounters/Triple Cultists|3 Cultists]]' triple Ritual, Rally, [[enemies/Book of Stabbing]]) make it obvious — a slow deck pays multiples, a burst deck a fraction — but the same term drives every fight's price: estimate turns-to-kill FIRST, then run the clock.
- **What to Do at Low HP**: Take a different path (event/rest/shop). If forced into a fight, use ALL potions turn 1. After any fight ends below 30%, next room MUST be non-combat. Low HP does NOT change combat behavior — you should already be full blocking, and the potion check is already part of every block plan (not something reserved for lethal turns).
- **The [[acts/Act 2]] Death Spiral**: Enter Act 2 at reasonable HP → take unnecessary damage (not full blocking) → enter next fight at critical HP → die. After ANY fight below 30%, next room must be non-combat. If map doesn't offer this, run was lost at map selection.
- **What Causes HP Drain** (the structures, not prices): multi-hit patterns under [[debuffs/Frail]]/[[debuffs/Weak]] you didn't strip; scalers outpacing a slow deck ([[enemies/Byrd]] flocks, ramping elites); heal-armed fights fed by spread chip ([[enemies/Mystic]]); high-block walls ground down by attrition when a burst line existed ([[enemies/Spheric Guardian]]); potions left unspent while unblocked damage went through; Decay curse compound damage; max-HP losses (Vampires).
- **Act 2 Preparation**: Enter at full HP. Upgrades matter more (upgrade over rest when above 50%). Healing sustain is critical — [[relics/Burning Blood]] cannot compensate for Act 2's fight clocks; aim for two healing sources (not counting rest sites) by early Act 2 (see [[layer:heuristics, drafting]]).
- **[[acts/Act 3]] runs the same ledger with bigger clocks**: hallway fights deal roughly double their Act 1 equivalents per turn, elites ([[enemies/Giant Head]], [[enemies/Reptomancer]], [[enemies/Nemesis]]) and the boss top the scale. Derive against the pages' damage clocks and keep more margin — recovery rooms are scarcer. ([[relics/Pantograph]]'s +25 HP at the boss fight's start belongs on the ledger as exactly +25, not as permission to skip the arithmetic.)
