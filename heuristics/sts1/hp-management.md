# HP Management

- **HP is an act-level resource, not per-fight.** "I can afford 8 damage" thinking is wrong. The goal in EVERY hallway fight is zero damage taken. HP thresholds below are for MAP PATHING decisions.
- **Resource ordering: HP > deck quality > gold > held potions.** Every fight is minmaxed for damage taken, not for resources conserved. Spending an expiring resource (a potion, a once-per-fight effect) to protect HP is the default trade, not a desperation move — see Potion Economy in [[layer:heuristics, combat]]. The greedy pattern (get through the fight cheaply, save everything) reads as efficient and loses runs: saved potions expire unused while chip damage compounds into a death two fights later.
- **Pathing is an expected-damage ledger, not a set of entry gates.** Every fight has an expected HP cost for a competently played line, and the per-enemy and boss pages state it (most easy hallway fights: 0-15 HP; hard hallways and elites: 25-45; bosses: 30-50, matchup-dependent). At high ascension shift the ranges up — Act 1 elites at A8+ run ~30-40 even played well — and prefer a page's ascension note over the generic range whenever one exists. The pathing question is arithmetic: current HP, minus the expected costs of every forced fight between here and the next recovery (rest site, shop, healing card), must stay above the largest single spike you could face along the way — with margin for a bad draw. If the subtraction goes negative, change the path or recover first. If no surviving path exists, the run was lost earlier, at map selection.
- **Why not entry thresholds:** a rule like "enter above 60%" is a cached version of that subtraction that throws away your deck, the rest of the path, and your recovery options. The cache misleads in both directions — it forbids cheap fights a burst deck wins cleanly at 45%, and it blesses expensive fights a slow deck shouldn't take at 75%. Worse, it teaches that HP only matters at checkpoints. Do the subtraction; the per-fight cost ranges are the knowledge worth keeping.
- **The hard-scaler exception checks the deck, not the HP bar.** Fights that scale ([[encounters/Triple Cultists|3 Cultists]]' triple Ritual, Rally, [[enemies/Book of Stabbing]]) are priced by your kill speed: a slow deck pays multiples of the listed cost no matter how high its HP, and a burst deck pays a fraction. Before routing into one, estimate turns-to-kill, then price the fight.
- **What to Do at Low HP**: Take a different path (event/rest/shop). If forced into fight below threshold, use ALL potions turn 1. After fight drops below 30%, next room MUST be non-combat. Low HP does NOT change combat behavior — you should already be full blocking, and the potion check is already part of every block plan (not something reserved for lethal turns).
- **The [[acts/Act 2]] Death Spiral**: Enter Act 2 at reasonable HP → take unnecessary damage (not full blocking) → enter next fight at critical HP → die. After ANY fight below 30%, next room must be non-combat. If map doesn't offer this, run was lost at map selection.
- **What Causes HP Drain**: [[enemies/Byrd]] fights (36-73 HP), Centurion+Mystic (25-61 HP), [[enemies/Chosen]] (30-40 HP), Snake Plant (15-42 HP), [[enemies/Spheric Guardian]] (30-41 HP), no healing between fights, potions left unspent while unblocked damage went through, Decay curse compound damage, Vampires event Max HP loss.
- **Act 2 Preparation**: Enter at full HP. Upgrades matter more (upgrade over rest when above 50%). Healing sustain is critical — [[relics/Burning Blood]] cannot compensate for 30-50 HP fights; aim for two healing sources (not counting rest sites) by early Act 2 (see [[layer:heuristics, drafting]]).
- **[[acts/Act 3]] runs the same ledger with bigger numbers**: hallway fights cost roughly double their Act 1 equivalents, elites ([[enemies/Giant Head]], [[enemies/Reptomancer]], [[enemies/Nemesis]]) and the boss sit at the top of the range. Re-read the per-enemy expected costs and keep more margin — recovery rooms are scarcer. ([[relics/Pantograph]]'s +25 HP at the boss fight's start belongs on the ledger as exactly +25, not as permission to skip the arithmetic.)
