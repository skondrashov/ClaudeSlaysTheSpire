# Nemesis

**Core rule:** The fight has a rhythm -- non-[[buffs/Intangible]] turns are your damage windows, Intangible turns are for setup/blocking. Do NOT waste damage cards on Intangible turns.

## Strategy

- Track the Intangible pattern: Turn 1 (no), Turn 2 (yes), Turn 3 (no), Turn 4 (yes), etc.
- On non-Intangible turns: deal maximum damage. Front-load burst here.
- On Intangible turns: block, setup, play Powers, apply debuffs
- Block for Scythe (45 damage). [[cards/Impervious]] is ideal. [[debuffs/Weak]] reduces it to 33.
- Apply Weak to survive Scythe turns -- without Weak, 45 damage is near-unsurvivable
- Burns management matters in long fights. Kill speed or exhaust tools to end before Burns overwhelm.
- [[debuffs/Poison]] is partially countered -- each tick only deals 1 on Intangible turns. Still works but takes twice as long.
- High-burst poison ([[cards/Catalyst]]) on a non-Intangible turn is still strong.

## Character Matchups

**[[characters/Silent]]:** [[cards/Wraith Form]] provides Intangible for YOU on dangerous Scythe turns. [[cards/Piercing Wail]] reduces the multi-hit (7x3 at A9). [[cards/Malaise]] reduces [[buffs/Strength]] for multiple turns. [[cards/Well-Laid Plans]] can save burst damage for non-Intangible turns.

**Danger Level:** High. Scythe (45 damage) can one-shot at low HP. Intangible doubles effective fight length. Burns clog draws. **At A9, HP is 200 and the multi-hit is 7x3.** Price this fight at the ROUTING node, not at fight entry: Intangible halving your poison/shiv clock + the 45 Scythe punching through a ~30 block ceiling make Nemesis a ~-40/-45 fight even when played cleanly and entered at full HP. Do not route into it off realized-average elite costs from earlier, lighter elites.
