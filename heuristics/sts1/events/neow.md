# Neow

The floor-0 blessing: four options drawn from pools — typically a safe small gain (max HP, an upgrade, a removal, potions, gold), a card option (card pick, rare card, transform), a gain-with-cost trade (pay HP or gold), and the boss-relic swap (trade the starter relic for a random boss relic). The pick sets the run's opening shape; spend a real decision on it.

The standard resource ordering applies here too: HP > deck quality > gold > held potions (see [[layer:heuristics, hp-management]]).

- **Max HP (+7/+8)** is the steadiest pick, and rises in value at high ascension — it is margin in every fight of the run.
- **A rare-card pick can outvalue max HP** when it lands an engine piece ([[cards/Immolate]]-class). Paying ~99 gold for it has been a good trade: floor-0 gold buys nothing before the first shop, while an early rare shapes the whole run.
- **Enemies-start-at-1-HP for three combats (Neow's Lament)** converts into three free fights, three free card rewards, and zero HP spent — route the first three floors into Monster rooms to cash every charge. The trap: full HP with an undeveloped deck does not make you elite-ready (see the Neow's Lament relic page).
- **The boss-relic swap** gambles away the starter relic — for [[characters/Ironclad]] that is [[relics/Burning Blood]], the only built-in healing — and can roll a downside relic ([[relics/Sozu]], [[relics/Cursed Key]]). Refuse it unless the run plan genuinely doesn't lean on the starter relic.
- **Taking damage for gold** violates HP > gold, and more so at high ascension where early fights already chip.
- **Upgrade / removal** are solid quiet picks when the better options above aren't offered ([[cards/Bash]]+ is the usual Ironclad upgrade target).

This page rests on few runs — treat the leanings as current best guesses, not law.
