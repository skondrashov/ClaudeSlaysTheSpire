# Awakened One (Act 3 Boss)

HP: Phase 1 = 300 (320 at A9). Phase 2 = 300. Regenerate 10 HP/turn in Phase 2. Two-phase fight. The Power-punishing boss.

DAMAGE:
- Slash: 20 damage
- Soul Strike: 6x4 = 24 damage
- Dark Echo: 40 damage (Phase 2 opener)
- Tackle: 10x3 = 30 damage (Phase 2)
- Sludge: 18 damage + adds Void card to deck (Phase 2)
- Rebirth: Full heal to 300 HP, removes player debuffs on boss, loses Curiosity

PATTERN (Phase 1): Opens with Slash. Then 75% Slash / 25% Soul Strike. Cannot use Slash 3x in a row or Soul Strike 2x in a row.
PATTERN (Phase 2): Opens with Dark Echo (40 damage). Then 50% Tackle / 50% Sludge. Cannot use either 3x in a row.

## THE CENTRAL MECHANIC: CURIOSITY

**Every time you play a Power card, the Awakened One gains +2 Strength.** This is not a footnote. This is the defining mechanic of the fight. It is the reason this boss exists. The developers created this boss specifically to punish Power-heavy decks.

**Do not rationalize playing Powers freely.** "My Powers are essential" is not an argument -- the mechanic exists precisely because Powers are good. The question is never "is this Power worth playing in a vacuum?" but "is this Power worth +2 Strength on a boss that already hits hard?" The answer is usually NO.

Each Power played adds +2 to every attack the Awakened One makes for the rest of that phase. Playing 3 Powers means +6 Strength. Playing 6 Powers means +12 Strength, turning a survivable 10x3 multi-attack into a lethal 22x3 = 66 damage hit. The Strength compounds across every remaining turn -- the earlier you play Powers, the more total damage you take.

## PHASE 1

The Awakened One starts with two Cultist minions (~48 HP each). Phase 1 has approximately 300 HP (A0). After Phase 1 HP reaches 0, the Awakened One enters Phase 2 with full HP (300). All your Powers are STRIPPED at the phase transition, but their EFFECTS (Dex from Footwork, block-per-card from After Image, Caltrops thorns) appear to persist (confirmed in 1 victory, needs more data). Poison on the boss is REMOVED.

**Phase 1 priorities:**
- Kill the Cultists quickly. They scale with Ritual (+1 Str/turn) and deal increasing damage if left alive.
- Damage the Awakened One to end Phase 1, but conserve HP for Phase 2.

**When to play Powers in Phase 1 -- THE DUMP STRATEGY (confirmed in victory):**
The Curiosity Strength gained in Phase 1 RESETS when the Awakened One dies and enters Phase 2. All player Powers are also stripped. This creates a unique strategic window: **play ALL your Powers in Phase 1, because the Strength cost resets on death.** The Powers help you kill Phase 1 faster (saving HP for Phase 2) and the Str given to the boss only applies to the remaining Phase 1 turns -- a short window.

This was executed successfully in a victory: After Image, Footwork x2, Well-Laid Plans, Caltrops, and Noxious Fumes were ALL played in Phase 1. The AO gained Str from Curiosity, but the player applied Terror (Vuln 99) and burst it down quickly. The Str cost was irrelevant because Phase 1 ended before it accumulated to dangerous levels.

**Phase 1 Power dump guidelines:**
- Play Powers early in Phase 1 to maximize their benefit before the phase transition strips them.
- Apply Terror/Vulnerable to convert the Power-boosted card plays into faster Phase 1 kills.
- Use Piercing Wail and Weak to counteract the Str the AO gains from Curiosity during Phase 1.
- The more Powers you play early, the faster Phase 1 ends, limiting the number of turns the AO gets to use its boosted Str.
- **This ONLY works because Phase 1 Str resets.** Phase 2 is the opposite -- see below.

## PHASE 2

HP: 300. Gains Regenerate 10 (heals 10 HP per turn). Curiosity resets -- Strength gains from Phase 1 Powers are gone, but the mechanic is ACTIVE AGAIN. Every Power you play in Phase 2 permanently buffs the boss for the rest of the fight.

**Phase 2 attack pattern:**
- Multi-attacks are the primary threat. A 10x3 base multi-attack becomes 16x3 = 48 with +6 Str, or 22x3 = 66 with +12 Str.
- The boss also uses single large attacks and debuff/buff turns.
- Regenerate 10 heals the boss every turn, so the fight has a clock -- you must out-damage the regeneration or you lose to attrition.

**Phase 2 Power discipline (STRICT -- confirmed by victory):**
- **0 Powers is the rule.** The victory run played ZERO Powers in Phase 2 and won. The player's hand frequently contained Powers (Noxious Fumes, Caltrops, Infinite Blades, etc.) that were deliberately not played. Instead, all damage came from Attacks and Skills + Dead Branch exhaust generation.
- **1 Power is the absolute maximum in desperate situations.** Only play it if it is your sole path to victory and you understand the Strength cost.
- **Every Power beyond the first makes the fight dramatically harder.** 2 Powers = +4 Str = roughly +12 extra damage per multi-attack turn. 3 Powers = +6 Str = +18 per multi-attack. This adds up across every remaining turn.
- **Dead Branch solves the "no Powers" problem.** Without Powers, the deck needs an alternative card generation engine. Dead Branch + exhaust cards (Terror, Piercing Wail, Adrenaline, Storm of Steel, Shivs, Endless Agony, Distraction) generates random cards every turn, providing both offense and defense without triggering Curiosity.

## HOW TO WIN WITHOUT POWERS

- **Poison (Silent):** Deadly Poison, Noxious Fumes (note: Noxious Fumes IS a Power -- consider whether the passive poison is worth +2 Str), Poison Stab, Bouncing Flask, Corpse Explosion. Catalyst+ triples poison, which defeats Regenerate 10. Prioritize poison applied by Skills and Attacks, not Powers.
- **Strength scaling (Ironclad):** Inflame, Spot Weakness (both are non-Power Str sources). Limit Break doubles existing Str. Heavy Blade leverages Str.
- **Front-loaded damage:** High-damage attacks (Immolate+, Carnage+, Glass Knife) applied early before the boss scales.
- **Block without Powers:** Impervious, Defend, Backflip, Blur, Leg Sweep, Footwork (Footwork IS a Power -- weigh the Dexterity against the Str cost).
- **Weak application:** Neutralize, Leg Sweep, Sucker Punch. Reducing the boss's damage by 25% is often worth more than a Power that adds 2 Str.

## WHAT KILLED US (Run 151 -- Silent, Phase 2 death)

Died Phase 2, Turn 14, 6 HP vs 16x3 = 48 multi-attack with only 28 block. The 16 per hit was 10 base + 6 Strength from Curiosity. Six Powers were played over the course of the fight, split across both phases. The Awakened One's Phase 2 Strength of 6 means 3 Powers were played in Phase 2 alone. Without those Phase 2 Powers, the multi-attack would have been 10x3 = 30, and 28 block would have left only 2 unblocked damage -- survivable at 6 HP.

The deck contained 7 Power cards: After Image+, Infinite Blades, Well-Laid Plans, Tools of the Trade, Accuracy, Footwork, Noxious Fumes. The player declared the Powers "essential" and played them freely. This is exactly the wrong approach.

## WHAT WON (Run 162 -- Silent, VICTORY, Dead Branch engine)

**Phase 1 -- Power dump:** Played ALL Powers early (After Image, Footwork x2, Well-Laid Plans, Caltrops, Noxious Fumes). Applied Terror for Vuln 99. Used Flex Potions + Glass Knife for 42-damage burst attacks. Burst damage + poison killed Phase 1 in ~8 turns. The Curiosity Str gained was offset by Piercing Wail (-6 Str) and Weak from Neutralize/Crippling Cloud. Phase 1 Str resets on death, so all Powers were "free."

**Phase 2 -- Zero Powers, Dead Branch engine:** Deliberately avoided all Power cards despite having them in hand. Damage came entirely from Attacks + Skills + Dead Branch random card generation. Key plays: Terror for Vuln 99 (exhaust = Dead Branch proc), Shivs from Cloak and Dagger exhausting for Dead Branch procs, Storm of Steel generating 3 Shivs that each exhausted for more Dead Branch procs, Adrenaline for free energy + Dead Branch proc, Distraction for Dead Branch proc + random Skill. Dex 7 (from Phase 1 Footwork x2 + Footwork+, which persisted -- Dex was NOT stripped) + After Image + Blur provided blocking. Lizard Tail saved the run on Phase 2 Turn 1 (41 damage vs 20 block + 18 HP). Final kill on ~Turn 7 of Phase 2 via Strike + Sucker Punch + Quick Slash burst.

**Key tactical insight: Dex from Footwork persisted into Phase 2.** Powers are stripped at the phase transition, but their EFFECTS (Dex, After Image block-per-card, Caltrops thorns) appear to persist. This means Phase 1 Power plays are doubly valuable -- the boss loses its Str, but the player keeps the buffs. Needs confirmation across more runs.

## DECK BUILDING FOR THIS FIGHT

When the Act 3 boss is Awakened One (you can see this on the map):
- **Without Dead Branch:** Deprioritize Power cards during drafting. Every Power in the deck is a card you must discipline yourself not to play in Phase 2, wasting a draw. Prioritize Skill-based and Attack-based scaling (Catalyst, Leg Sweep, Backflip).
- **With Dead Branch:** Powers are WELCOME in the deck. Play them ALL in Phase 1 (dump strategy) for their full benefit. In Phase 2, ignore them in hand and rely on Dead Branch + exhaust for card generation. The more exhaust cards in the deck (Terror, Piercing Wail, Adrenaline, Endless Agony, Storm of Steel, Distraction), the stronger the Dead Branch engine. Dead Branch fundamentally changes the Awakened One from the hardest boss to a manageable one.
- **Terror is the MVP card for this fight.** 0-1E for Vulnerable 99 (permanent 50% damage boost) + exhaust (Dead Branch trigger). Take Terror whenever offered if Awakened One is the Act 3 boss.
- **If your deck is Power-heavy without Dead Branch, this is the hardest Act 3 boss.** A deck with 5+ Powers that relies on those Powers to function will struggle badly.

## PREPARATION CHECKLIST

1. Damage source that works without Powers -- poison via Skills/Attacks, Strength from non-Power sources, raw attack damage.
2. Block density -- Impervious, Leg Sweep, Backflip, Defend+ all work without triggering Curiosity.
3. Weak application -- Neutralize, Leg Sweep reduce the multi-attack threat significantly.
4. Sustain vs Regenerate 10 -- must deal >10 net damage per turn averaged across the fight. Catalyst+ on poison solves this. High Str + multi-hit solves this.
5. Enter at 70%+ HP. Phase 2 is a long fight (10-15 turns) with escalating damage.

## DEAD BRANCH STRATEGY (confirmed victory)

Dead Branch + exhaust-heavy Silent deck is the best approach against the Awakened One because it generates card advantage in Phase 2 WITHOUT playing Powers. The engine:
1. **Phase 1:** Play ALL Powers (dump strategy). Apply Terror for Vuln 99, burst down Phase 1.
2. **Phase 2:** Play ZERO Powers. Use exhaust cards (Terror, Piercing Wail, Adrenaline, Storm of Steel, Endless Agony, Distraction, Crippling Cloud) for Dead Branch procs. Each proc generates a random card. Shivs from Cloak and Dagger exhaust for more procs, creating chain reactions.
3. **Block in Phase 2:** After Image (from Phase 1) provides +1 block per card played. Dex (from Phase 1 Footwork) boosts all Skill block. Blur carries block between turns. Combined, these provide 20-40 block per turn from Skills + After Image without playing any new Powers.
4. **Damage in Phase 2:** Terror Vuln 99 makes all attacks deal 50% more. Shivs at (4 base + Dex) * 1.5 Vuln = 15+ each. Random Dead Branch cards contribute attacks. Storm of Steel -> Shiv chain for 45+ burst damage.

## POTION TIMING

- Save at least 1 potion for Phase 2. Phase 2 is the harder phase -- do not spend everything in Phase 1.
- Weak Potion on multi-attack turns reduces lethal hits.
- Block Potion on lethal turns provides emergency survival.
- Poison-applying potions bypass the Power restriction entirely.
