# Flex (0E, Skill, gain 2 Strength this turn | +: gain 4 Strength this turn)
Temporary Strength that disappears at end of turn. Effectively +2 (or +4) damage to each Attack played THIS TURN only.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Almost never. The temporary nature means it only adds value if you play multiple attacks on the same turn AND the extra damage matters for a kill threshold. In theory, 0E for +4 damage across 3 attacks = 12 extra damage. In practice, it dilutes the deck by occupying a draw slot in every subsequent turn without providing lasting value.

WHY IT'S BAD: Flex takes a card draw slot every time it cycles through the deck. In fights that generate status cards (Book of Stabbing Wounds, Hexaghost Burns, Slime Boss Slimed), every non-essential card in the deck compounds the clog problem. Flex provides zero block, zero permanent scaling, and zero utility outside the single turn it is played. A deck with Flex is strictly worse than the same deck without it in any fight lasting 4+ turns.

DECISION POINTS: Do not add Flex to the deck. If already in deck (from Neow transform, shop misclick, or event), remove at next shop opportunity. If drawn during combat, play it turn 1 if you have 2+ attacks to follow, otherwise it wastes a draw slot for 0 value.

COMPARISON: Inflame costs 1E but provides +2 permanent Strength. Over a 5-turn fight playing 2 attacks/turn, Inflame adds 20 total damage. Flex adds 4-8 damage one time. Inflame is 3-5x better in any fight lasting more than 1 turn.

AVOID: Taking from card rewards, buying from shops. This card should never be in an Ironclad deck voluntarily.
