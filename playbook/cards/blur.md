# Blur

Silent Skill, 1E.

**Effect:** Gain 5 Block (8 upgraded). Block is not removed at the start of your next turn.

**Base:** 5 block + block retention.
**Upgraded:** 8 block + block retention.

## Key Mechanic: Block Retention

Blur allows block to carry over to the next turn. This is critical in two scenarios:

1. **Free turns (enemy buffing/debuffing):** When the enemy is not attacking, any block generated is normally wasted. With Blur, block from a free turn carries into the next attack turn. Against the Collector's DEFEND_BUFF turns, this means block generated during the buff turn persists to the next attack turn.

2. **Wraith Form Intangible turns:** During Intangible, you take only 1 damage regardless of the attack value. Any block generated is wasted unless Blur is active. With Blur, block from Intangible turns carries over to post-Intangible turns when the Dex penalty makes generating new block difficult or impossible.

## Wraith Form Synergy

Blur is the critical companion card to Wraith Form:
- Intangible turns: Generate block freely (enemy damage reduced to 1). Blur carries this block to the next turn.
- Post-Intangible turns: Dex penalty means new block is minimal. Carried-over block from the Intangible phase covers the gap.
- Without Blur, Wraith Form's Intangible turns provide defense but the transition to post-Intangible is a cliff -- block drops from "infinite" to near-zero.

## When to Play

- **On free turns (DEFEND_BUFF, setup turns):** Generate block and carry it forward.
- **On the last Intangible turn:** Maximize block on the final safe turn so it carries into the first dangerous turn.
- **When After Image is generating excess block:** If After Image + card plays generate more block than needed this turn, Blur preserves the surplus.

## Draft Priority

Blur is a mid-priority card for the Silent. It becomes high-priority when the deck includes Wraith Form, as the two cards form a strong defensive package together. Without Wraith Form, Blur is a decent but not essential block card (5/8 block for 1E is below average, but the retention effect adds conditional value).
