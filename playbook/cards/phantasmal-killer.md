# Phantasmal Killer

Silent Power, 1E (0E upgraded).

**Effect:** Your next Attack deals double damage.

**Base:** 1E, double next Attack.
**Upgraded:** 0E, double next Attack.

## Key Mechanic

Phantasmal Killer grants the "Double Damage" buff, which doubles the damage of the NEXT Attack card played. This includes all modifiers (Strength, Accuracy, Vulnerable). The buff persists until an Attack is played -- it can be set up one turn and used the next.

## Optimal Timing

- **Play the turn BEFORE a burst turn.** On a free turn (enemy buffing/defending), play Phantasmal Killer. Next turn, play the highest-value Attack for doubled damage.
- **With Shivs:** Each Shiv is an Attack. With Accuracy x2 (+8) and Str scaling, a doubled Shiv deals 36+ damage. Play PK, then Blade Dance next turn for 3 doubled Shivs.
- **Trigger Time Warp intentionally:** If the 12-card counter is at 11/12, play Phantasmal Killer as the 12th card on a free turn. Time Warp triggers, but Double Damage persists for the fresh cycle's first attack.
- **With Vulnerable:** PK doubles after Vulnerable multiplier. A 12-damage Shiv with Vulnerable = 18, doubled = 36.

## Synergies

- **Blade Dance:** 3 Shivs, each doubled = devastating burst.
- **Accuracy:** +4/+8 per Shiv, all doubled.
- **Finisher:** After 3-4 doubled Shivs, Finisher's damage (based on attacks played this turn) is also doubled.
- **Flechettes+:** Counts Skills in hand, doubled = 24+ damage for 1E.
- **All-Out Attack+:** AOE attack, doubled = massive multi-target damage.
- **Fear Potion/Vulnerable:** Multiplicative with Double Damage.

## Victory-Proven Sequence

First Silent victory, Turn 3 vs Time Eater: Phantasmal Killer (played Turn 2) + Fear Potion (3 Vulnerable) + 2 Shivs at 36 each (doubled, Vuln) = 72 burst damage from 2 card plays. This combination of setup (PK) + multiplier (Vuln) + efficient damage (Shivs with Accuracy) is the core burst pattern for the Silent Shiv archetype.

## Draft Priority

High priority when the deck has Blade Dance and/or Accuracy. The upgraded version (0E) is strictly better -- it costs nothing and can be played on any turn as a free setup.
