# Barricade (3E, Power, block is not removed at start of turn)

Block persists between turns instead of resetting to 0. Transforms block cards from temporary defense into permanent HP shields.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: In block-heavy decks with multiple block sources (Shrug It Off, True Grit+, Defend, Iron Wave). Once played, every point of block generated for the rest of the fight is permanent. Combined with Body Slam, accumulated block becomes a repeatable damage source. Strongest in long fights (bosses, elites) where the setup cost is amortized over many turns.

DECISION POINTS: At 3E, playing Barricade costs an entire turn (3E budget). You generate ZERO block and deal ZERO damage on the turn you play it. This is only acceptable if: (1) the enemy is buffing or defending (free turn), (2) you are at high enough HP to absorb one unblocked attack, or (3) you have 4+ energy from relics or Offering. NEVER play Barricade when the incoming attack will kill you or leave you at critical HP. The setup cost is the card's biggest weakness.

SYNERGIES: Body Slam (damage = current block -- with Barricade, this scales every turn), Shrug It Off (8 block + draw, permanent with Barricade), True Grit+ (9 block + exhaust, permanent), Iron Wave (5 block + 5 damage), Metallicize (3-4 passive block/turn becomes permanent), Rage (block from attacks persists -- but Rage itself expires at end of turn, so only the block generated on the Rage turn persists).

ANTI-SYNERGIES: Low energy decks (3E cost is prohibitive without extra energy), short fights (not enough turns to recover the setup cost), fights at low HP (cannot afford a turn without block). Brutality (the fight extension from Barricade's defensive style compounds Brutality's self-damage).

MATCHUPS: Spheric Guardian -- the ENEMY has Barricade, not you. If you have Barricade yourself, the fight still requires massive burst to break through the enemy's persistent block. Your own Barricade helps survive but does not solve the DPS problem.

GUARDIAN SYNERGY (CONFIRMED): Barricade + Impervious = 30 permanent block in one play. Against the Guardian, deploy Barricade on a free turn (DEFEND or STRONG_DEBUFF intent), then play Impervious on a subsequent turn to build a permanent block wall. Observed: Barricade played on Turn 4 (DEFEND intent, took 12 unblocked at 59 HP), then Impervious on Turn 8 created 36 permanent block. Combined with Shrug It Off and Defend, the player accumulated enough permanent block to absorb all remaining Guardian attacks and win. The Guardian fight's alternating free turns make Barricade's 3E setup cost manageable.

ACT 3 ENGINE (CONFIRMED): Barricade+ (2E) + 2x Metallicize (3+4 = 7 block/turn stacking) is the strongest sustained defense engine observed. Against The Maw (300 HP, escalating damage 5/16/33/56), this engine resulted in zero damage taken across 9 turns. The block snowball accelerates: Turn 3 has ~15 carried block, Turn 5 has ~25-30, and by Turn 7 incoming damage cannot exceed accumulated block. Pair with Inflame+ for damage and the deck handles any Act 3 hallway fight. Against The Transient (escalating 30/40/50/60/70), Barricade + Metallicize makes turns 4-5 comfortable even without Impervious.

NOTE: Against multi-enemy fights with constant incoming damage (Gremlin Leader, 3 Cultists), the 3E setup cost is often too expensive. Barricade shares the same 3E setup cost problem as Corruption -- both consume an entire turn with no immediate block or damage. See Corruption entry for detailed analysis of the setup cost trap in multi-enemy fights. At 5E (from Fusion Hammer or Ectoplasm), Barricade+ costs only 2E, leaving 3E for block cards -- this eliminates the setup cost problem entirely.
