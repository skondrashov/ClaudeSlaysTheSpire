# Flame Barrier (2E, Skill, 12 block + 4 counter damage per hit | +: 16 block + 6 counter per hit)
Block + thorns effect. Punishes multi-hit attacks.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Excellent against multi-hit enemies. Flame Barrier+ against Hexaghost Inferno (7 hits) = 16 block + 42 counter damage. Against multi-enemy fights (Slavers, Gremlins), each attacker that hits triggers counter damage.

DECISION POINTS: At 2E, competes with other defensive plays. Worth it when facing multi-hit attacks or multiple attackers. On single-hit attack turns, a Defend (1E, 5 block) may be more energy-efficient.

UPGRADE: 12->16 block, 4->6 counter per hit. High upgrade priority when facing Hexaghost (42 counter from Inferno). The block increase alone (12->16) is worth upgrading.

SYNERGIES:
- **Hexaghost Inferno**: 7 hits x 6 counter = 42 free damage, plus 16 block absorbs most of the 28 weakened Inferno damage. Single best defensive card for Inferno turns.
- **Multi-enemy fights**: Each enemy that attacks triggers counter damage. Against Slavers (3 attackers), Flame Barrier+ deals 18 counter damage (6 per attacker) while blocking 16.
- **Gremlin fights**: Multiple small attackers each trigger 4-6 counter damage.

MATCHUPS: Best-in-class vs Hexaghost. Strong vs Book of Stabbing -- multi-hit attacks trigger counter damage on every hit, and hit count increases each turn (turn 4 = 5 hits x 4-6 counter = 20-30 free damage). Strong in multi-enemy Act 2 fights. Moderate vs single large attackers (block is good but counter only triggers once).
