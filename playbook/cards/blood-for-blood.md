# Blood for Blood (4E, Attack, 18 damage | +: 22 damage, costs 1 less for each HP lost this combat)

Starts at 4 Energy but becomes cheaper as you take damage. After losing enough HP in a fight, Blood for Blood costs 0 Energy -- free 18 (or 22) damage.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: In fights where you take early damage (most Act 2 fights). After losing 4+ HP, Blood for Blood becomes a 0-cost 18-damage Attack -- one of the most energy-efficient plays in the game. Excellent burst when combined with Double Tap (0E for 36-44 damage). Strong in boss fights where taking some damage is inevitable.

DECISION POINTS: At 0 cost, always play it. At 1-2 cost, evaluate whether the damage justifies the energy. At 3-4 cost (start of fight before taking damage), hold it and play other cards first. The cost reduction is permanent for the combat -- there is no rush to play it immediately.

SYNERGIES: Double Tap (double a 0-cost Attack = 36+ free damage), Inflame/Strength (18 base + Str, amplified by Vulnerable), Bludgeon (both are high-damage single-hits that pair well in burst turns).

WEAKNESSES: Terrible on turn 1 of fights where you block all damage (costs 4E if you take no hits). In decks that prevent all damage (Barricade + heavy block), the cost may never reduce. Not reliable as your only damage source -- need other cards for turns before the cost drops.
