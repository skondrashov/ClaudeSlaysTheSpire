# Havoc (0E, Skill, play the top card of your draw pile and exhaust it)

Plays the top card of the draw pile for free, then exhausts it. The played card's energy cost is ignored (free), but the card is permanently lost.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: When the draw pile contains expendable cards (Strikes, status cards, Wounds, Burns, Dazed) and you want a free action. The 0-energy cost makes it tempo-positive. Best when you know or can predict what is on top (e.g., after Headbutt).

CRITICAL DANGER: Havoc exhausts the played card permanently. If the top card is a key scaling card (Rampage+, Fiend Fire+, Bash+), it is gone forever. In a deck with even one critical card you cannot afford to lose, Havoc is a coin flip that can end the run. Havoc exhausted Rampage+ (the primary damage scaling card) during a Spheric Guardian fight, directly contributing to a run loss two fights later.

DECISION POINTS:
- Before playing, consider what is in the draw pile. If Rampage+, Fiend Fire+, Feed+, or any irreplaceable card could be on top, DO NOT PLAY HAVOC.
- Havoc is safe when: (1) draw pile is mostly Strikes/Defends, (2) you tracked what was drawn and know what remains, (3) the fight is trivial and losing any card does not matter.
- Havoc is dangerous when: (1) deck contains high-value exhaust-sensitive cards, (2) you have not tracked draw pile contents, (3) the run depends on a specific card being available in future fights.

SYNERGIES: Headbutt (place a specific card on top, then Havoc plays it for free -- controlled combo). Status-heavy hand (free action that might hit a real card).

ANTI-SYNERGY: Any deck with irreplaceable scaling cards (Rampage, Fiend Fire, Feed). The random exhaust risk outweighs the 0-energy benefit. DO NOT take Havoc if the deck depends on specific cards surviving to future fights.

MATCHUPS: Gremlin Nob -- Havoc is a Skill, triggers Enrage. Do NOT play.
