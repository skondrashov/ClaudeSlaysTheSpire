# Inflame (1E, Power, gain 2 Strength permanently)
Cheap, permanent damage scaling. Every Attack deals 2 more damage for the entire fight.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Play as early as possible in long fights (bosses, elites) for maximum value. At 1E cost, extremely efficient.
DECISION POINTS: Is this turn a setup turn or a survival turn? On free turns (enemy buffing/debuffing), always play Inflame. On attack turns with high incoming damage (Hexaghost Turn 2: ~30 damage), Inflame is still worth playing BUT do not stack multiple setup cards (Inflame + Spot Weakness + Ghostly Armor = 3E with minimal block). Play Inflame (1E) and use remaining 2E on block. Damage formula with Inflame + Weak: floor((base+2)*0.75). Powers do NOT trigger Gremlin Nob Enrage.
SYNERGIES: Reaper (Strength increases damage to ALL enemies = more healing), Heavy Blade (+2 Str becomes +6 damage, or +10 upgraded, due to multiplier), Whirlwind (each hit gains +2 damage), Twin Strike (+2 per hit = +4 total), every Attack card.
MATCHUPS: Guardian Defensive Mode -- Inflame is a Power, not an Attack, so no Sharp Hide retaliation. Bronze Automaton -- Play Inflame immediately when drawn to avoid Stasis theft. Inflame+ alone (+3 Str) is functional but marginal against 300 HP -- the fight took 13 turns and failed by 7 HP. For Bronze Automaton, Inflame+ ideally pairs with a second Strength source (Spot Weakness, Strength Potion, or Demon Form) to kill before the second Hyper Beam cycle. Having two copies provides insurance against one being stolen. Spheric Guardian -- CRITICAL. Without Strength scaling, this fight is mathematically unwinnable (Barricade block regenerates faster than chip damage removes it). Inflame is the single best card for breaking through Spheric Guardian's defenses. Take Inflame over most other card rewards if no Strength scaling exists in the deck.
