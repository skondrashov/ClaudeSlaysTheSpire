# Power Through (1E, Skill, 15 block, adds 2 Wounds to hand)
Massive block (triple Defend) but pollutes hand with 2 unplayable Wounds.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Must-survive turns with massive incoming damage. When you have exhaust tools (True Grit+, Fiend Fire, Second Wind) to remove the Wounds afterward. Short fights (3-4 turns) where the Wounds won't cycle back.
WHEN IT'S A TRAP: In long fights (Shelled Parasite, scaling enemies) without exhaust tools. The Wounds reduce effective hand size, meaning fewer block AND fewer damage cards per turn. Over an 8+ turn fight, playing Power Through twice adds 4 Wounds to the deck, reducing block density for all subsequent turns. This creates a paradox: you took Power Through for block, but the Wounds reduce your future blocking ability.
DECISION POINTS: Is 15 block worth 2 dead draws in future hands? In long fights without exhaust tools, the Wounds accumulate and ruin draws. In short fights or with exhaust, the 15 block is worth it.
DRAFT RULE: Do not take Power Through unless the deck has at least one exhaust tool (True Grit+, Fiend Fire, Second Wind, Burning Pact) or the deck is fast enough to end fights in 4-5 turns.
