# Gremlin Nob (elite, Act 1, HP: 82-86)

PATTERN: Always starts with Bellow (gains Enrage 2). After that: 33% Skull Bash, 67% Rush. Cannot use Rush three times in a row.

DAMAGE:
- Bellow: Gains Enrage 2 (whenever you play a Skill, Nob gains 2 Strength)
- Skull Bash: 6 damage + 2 Vulnerable (8 at A2)
- Rush (Bull Rush): 14 damage (16 at A2)

HP: 82-86 at A0, 85-90 at A8

KEY MECHANIC: Enrage 2. Every Skill card played gives Nob +2 Strength permanently. Powers do NOT trigger Enrage.
WHAT THIS MEANS: Play ZERO Skills. No Defend, no True Grit, no Shrug It Off, no Shockwave, no Impervious. Only Attacks and Powers. Use potions for block instead of Skill cards. Turn 1 is free -- go all-in on damage.
BENCHMARKS: 3-4 turn kill with zero Skills. Strength Potion on free turn 1 maximizes value. Playing 3 Skills gives Nob +6 Str, turning a manageable fight into near-death.
BRING: Attack-heavy hand. Bash (Attack, safe). Potions for block/survival instead of Skills.
AVOID: ANY Skill card. The difference is massive: zero Skills = clean kill, 3 Skills = near-death.
DECK COMPOSITION WARNING: Before pathing into a potential Nob fight, count Attacks vs Skills in the deck. If the deck has more Skills than Attacks (e.g., 7 Skills vs 4 Attacks), most hands will contain 3-4 Skills and only 1-2 Attacks. Even with perfect discipline (play only Attacks, discard Skills), the kill takes 7+ turns because each hand only has 1-2 playable cards. By Turn 5-6, Nob's scaling makes incoming damage 20-30+, and the player is forced to play Skills for survival, compounding the Str gain. A Skill-heavy deck makes Nob a near-unwinnable fight.

SILENT-SPECIFIC WARNING: The Silent's starter deck and most early picks (Defend x5, Survivor, Backflip, Leg Sweep, Piercing Wail, Blade Dance) are Skills. A typical early Silent deck has 9+ Skills vs 5 Attacks. Even "careful" play forces 5-7 Skill triggers across a 7-turn fight because: (a) pure-Attack hands are rare (2-3 Attacks in 5-card draw), (b) survival requires playing block Skills when Nob attacks for 20+, (c) Blade Dance generates Shiv Attacks but is itself a Skill that triggers Enrage. Confirmed: a Silent deck at full HP played 7 Skills across 7 turns, giving Nob +14 Str, costing 28 HP. When Hexaghost is the boss, do NOT waste Piercing Wail on Nob -- save it for Inferno. Use Strength Potion, Explosive Potion, or Fire Potion for Nob damage instead of Skill-based defense.

DEFECT-SPECIFIC WARNING: The Defect has the most Skill-heavy starting deck of any character: 6 Skills (4 Defend + Zap + Dualcast) vs 4 Attacks (4 Strike). Adding Coolheaded (a common early pickup) makes it 7 Skills vs 4 Attacks. Both Zap (0E) and Dualcast (1E) are Skills that trigger Enrage. Gremlin Nob is the Defect's WORST elite matchup. Do NOT path into a Nob fight unless the deck has picked up multiple Attack cards (Compile Driver, Sweeping Beam, Ball Lightning, etc.) to rebalance the ratio. Even with Horn Cleat providing 14 free block on Turn 2, the fight drains most of the Defect's HP at early floors.
