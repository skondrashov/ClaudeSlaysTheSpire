# Chosen (hallway, Act 2, HP: 95-99)

PATTERN: Always starts with Poke, then Hex. After that, repeats: (Debilitate 50% / Drain 50%) followed by (Poke 60% / Zap 40%).

DAMAGE:
- Poke: 5x2 = 10 damage (6x2 = 12 at A2)
- Zap: 18 damage (21 at A2)
- Debilitate: 10 damage + 2 Vulnerable (12 at A2)
- Drain: 0 damage, applies 3 Weak + gains 3 Strength
- Hex: Applies Hex debuff (whenever you play a non-Attack card, shuffle 1 Dazed into draw pile)

KEY MECHANIC: Hex. Every non-Attack card played adds a Dazed to draw pile. Blocking is actively costly -- each Defend clogs the deck. Also applies Vulnerable to player, making Zap (18) and Poke (10) hit harder.
WHAT THIS MEANS: Minimize Skill usage. Front-load damage with Attacks. Bash+ is safe (Attack). Kill before Strength buffs make 7x2 lethal. With Vulnerable on you, even 7x2 base becomes 20 damage per turn. At low HP (<20), the 7x2 with Vulnerable is a death sentence.

HP DRAIN: This fight routinely drains 30-40 HP even with a strong deck. If opening hand lacks block cards, the first 1-2 turns of unblocked 10-14 damage create immediate danger. At 50 HP entry, leaving at 17 HP is a realistic outcome -- which then makes the NEXT fight lethal (the death spiral pattern). Confirmed: (1) 39 HP entry with Carnage+, Bash+, Headbutt, Dropkick, Demon Form, and Ghostly Armor resulted in 32 HP drain (39->7 HP) over 7 turns. Even with Demon Form scaling, the Chosen's Hex penalty on Skills and Vulnerable on the player made every turn painful. (2) Silent Cultist+Chosen at 30 HP entry with strong Shiv deck (ATC, Envenom, Footwork, Blade Dance+, 2x CnD+) resulted in 26 HP drain (30->4 HP) over 7 turns -- Hex added 5+ Dazed by Turn 4, collapsing block density. The fight is a reliable death spiral trigger in Act 2.

BENCHMARKS: Kill in 4-5 turns. Damage output drops from Weak -- plan accordingly. Must enter with 40+ HP minimum.
BRING: Attack-heavy deck. Iron Wave (Attack that blocks). Inflame (Strength overcomes Weak penalty). Bash+ (Attack, applies Vulnerable). Metallicize (passive block without triggering Hex -- Power, not a Skill).
AVOID: Playing Defend unless absolutely necessary (each one adds Dazed). Slow kill (Strength scaling is lethal). Entering below 30 HP (7x2 + Vulnerable = 20/turn is often unblockable at low card density).

FIEND FIRE WARNING: Fiend Fire can exhaust Dazed cards for damage, but it also exhausts ALL other cards in hand -- including defensive cards like Uppercut and Defend. If Fiend Fire does not kill the Chosen, the remaining turns have no block sources. At 14 HP, a single 28-damage attack with only 13 block available is lethal. Only use Fiend Fire when the burst will kill, or when the hand contains only expendable cards.
APPEARS WITH: Cultist (in multi-enemy fights). When paired, Cultist buffs turn 1 while Chosen applies Hex -- the combined threat is extreme.

KILL PRIORITY: **Kill Chosen FIRST.** Previous guidance said "kill Cultist first" but three deaths from Hex deck flooding prove this wrong. The Cultist's Ritual scaling is dangerous but PREDICTABLE (damage grows linearly). Hex's Dazed flooding is CATASTROPHIC -- every Skill played after Turn 2 adds a Dazed to the draw pile, and by Turn 4-5 the deck is so diluted that critical turns draw 2-3 Dazed cards and only 1-2 playable cards. Killing the Cultist first leaves Chosen alive for 4+ turns applying Hex, which destroys block density. Killing Chosen first stops Hex immediately, and the Cultist alone (even with +6-9 Ritual Str) is a simple single-target fight.

EXCEPTION: If the Cultist can be killed in 1-2 turns (Fire Potion burst, Shiv flood, or it has <15 HP), killing it first is acceptable because Hex only applies for 1-2 turns. If the Cultist will take 3+ turns to kill, kill Chosen first.

CONFIRMED: Three deaths from killing Cultist first while Hex flooded the deck (1 Ironclad, 2 Silent). The Dazed accumulation made blocking impossible by Turn 5-6 in all cases.

HEX DETAILS: Hex is applied turn 2 (after Poke on turn 1). Each Skill played (Defend, Shrug It Off, etc.) adds one Dazed card to the draw pile. Dazed is Unplayable + Ethereal (exhausts at end of turn if in hand, but still takes up a draw slot). Over a 4-5 turn fight, playing 4-6 Skills adds 4-6 Dazed to the draw pile, significantly diluting future hands. This makes sustained blocking impossible -- the deck clogs with dead draws. Powers (Inflame, Rage, Footwork, After Image, A Thousand Cuts) do NOT trigger Hex. Attacks are safe.

SILENT-SPECIFIC WARNING: Hex is disproportionately devastating for the Silent. Silent decks are naturally Skill-heavy (Backflip, Footwork, Survivor, Deflect, Blur, Piercing Wail, Acrobatics). In a Cultist+Chosen fight, Hex can add 5+ Dazed cards by Turn 4, diluting the deck to the point where critical turns draw 3-4 Dazed cards and 1-2 playable cards. The block density collapse is lethal when combined with Chosen's Vulnerable. Confirmed across 3 deaths: (1) deck with Footwork+, After Image, A Thousand Cuts, Envenom (all Powers, safe from Hex) still needed 3 Defends on Turn 3 (adding 3 Dazed), then Turn 4 drew too few block cards to survive. (2) Shiv deck with Finisher+, Terror+, WLP+, Adrenaline entered at 15 HP, killed Cultist first (Turn 3), but Hex added 7+ Dazed by Turn 6. Hand on death turn: 2 Dazed + 1 Strike + 2 Defend = 10 block vs 27 incoming. Curses (Doubt + Injury from Du-Vu Doll strategy) compounded the draw dilution. Silent mitigation: (1) **Kill Chosen FIRST** -- see KILL PRIORITY above. (2) Play Powers liberally -- they do NOT trigger Hex. (3) Front-load damage with Attacks and Shivs (Blade Dance, Cloak and Dagger -- the block from CnD is a Skill but the Shivs are Attacks). (4) Use Piercing Wail Turn 1 BEFORE Hex is applied to negate damage for a free turn. (5) Avoid curse-heavy decks (Du-Vu Doll strategy) when Hex encounters are likely -- curses compound draw dilution with Dazed.
