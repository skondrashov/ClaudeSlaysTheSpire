# Gremlin Leader (elite, Act 2, HP: ~131)
PATTERN: Summons Sneaky Gremlins (~10-12 HP each). Uses DEFEND_BUFF (Rally): gives ALL enemies +3 Strength permanently and stacking. Gremlins attack for ~9 base + accumulated Str. Leader itself attacks for significant damage when not rallying. Leader re-summons gremlins when they die -- killing gremlins is temporary relief, not permanent removal.
KEY MECHANIC: Rally gives +3 Str to ALL enemies (Leader + all gremlins). Permanent and stacking. After 2 rallies: every enemy has +6 Str. The fight's damage scales exponentially with turn count. With +3 Str after one rally, gremlins deal 9+3=12 each. With two gremlins attacking, that is 24 damage from gremlins alone, plus the Leader's own attacks. Second data point: at 54% HP entry (43/80), turn 3 total enemy damage was 44 (mathematically unblockable with a 19-card deck containing 5 Strikes). Even with FNP + Shockwave+ + Fiend Fire+ burst tools, the fight ended in death because gremlins + Leader combined output exceeded all available block. The fight is unwinnable at low HP because there is no time to set up before Rally scaling makes incoming damage lethal.
WHAT THIS MEANS: KILL GREMLINS FAST to reduce per-turn damage, but prioritize Leader damage when possible because gremlins get re-summoned. AOE is critical for clearing gremlins efficiently. Apply Shockwave early for mass Weak + Vulnerable. The fight becomes mathematically unwinnable once Strength stacks too high (by turn 3-4 with multiple gremlins alive, incoming damage is 40+).
BENCHMARKS: Must win by turn 5-6 or Rally scaling overwhelms you. Enter with 60%+ HP minimum -- 54% (43/80) was insufficient even with strong burst tools. At 25% HP (14/56), the fight is unwinnable even with Nilry's Codex generating Impervious and Immolate. Bloated decks (19 cards with 5 Strikes) cannot draw answers fast enough against Rally's exponential scaling.
BRING: AOE (Thunderclap, Whirlwind, Immolate+), Shockwave+ (mass debuff), Disarm (reduce Leader Str). Non-exhaust damage cards for sustained fighting. Rage (0E power that generates block per attack -- excellent value in multi-attack turns). Potions -- this fight demands aggressive potion use.
AVOID: Entering below 60% HP. Exhaust-heavy decks (once Impervious/Shockwave/Disarm are gone, remaining deck is too weak). If below 50% HP, take any alternative path. Decks with reduced Max HP (e.g., post-Vampires at 56 Max HP) have no margin for error against this fight's scaling.
