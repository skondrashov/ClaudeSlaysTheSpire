# Gremlin Leader (elite, Act 2, HP: ~131)
PATTERN: Summons Sneaky Gremlins (~10-12 HP each). Uses DEFEND_BUFF (Rally): gives ALL enemies +3 Strength permanently and stacking. Gremlins attack for ~9 base + accumulated Str. Leader itself attacks for significant damage when not rallying. Leader re-summons gremlins when they die -- killing gremlins is temporary relief, not permanent removal.
KEY MECHANIC: Rally gives +3 Str to ALL enemies (Leader + all gremlins). Permanent and stacking. After 2 rallies: every enemy has +6 Str. The fight's damage scales exponentially with turn count. With +3 Str after one rally, gremlins deal 9+3=12 each. With two gremlins attacking, that is 24 damage from gremlins alone, plus the Leader's own attacks.
RALLY SCALING MATH: Rally fires roughly every other turn. By turn 3, all enemies have +3 Str. By turn 5, all enemies have +6 Str. By turn 7, all enemies have +9 Str. With 2 gremlins alive at +9 Str, gremlins alone deal (9+9)*2 = 36, plus the Leader's own attacks. Total incoming by turn 7-8 exceeds 50 per turn. Every wasted turn is effectively +3 Str to the entire board -- a single lost turn (e.g., ending turn early after Battle Trance draw without playing drawn cards) can be the difference between winning and losing.
WHAT THIS MEANS: TURN ECONOMY IS EVERYTHING. Every turn must deal maximum damage or apply critical debuffs. Wasting even one turn (misplaying end-of-turn, failing to use drawn cards) allows Rally to stack once more, which compounds across all gremlins. KILL GREMLINS FAST to reduce per-turn damage, but prioritize Leader damage when possible because gremlins get re-summoned. AOE is critical for clearing gremlins efficiently. Apply Shockwave early for mass Weak + Vulnerable. The fight becomes mathematically unwinnable once Strength stacks too high (by turn 3-4 with multiple gremlins alive, incoming damage is 40+).
BENCHMARKS: Must win by turn 5-6 or Rally scaling overwhelms you. Enter with 60%+ HP minimum AND at least 1 potion. At 55% HP (44/80) with zero potions and no Strength scaling, died on turn 4 to 47 incoming damage (Leader 12x3=36 + Mad Gremlin 11). At 56% (56/98) was insufficient even with Corruption + Feel No Pain engine. At 25% HP (14/56), unwinnable even with Nilry's Codex. Bloated decks (19+ cards with 4+ Strikes) cannot draw answers fast enough against Rally's exponential scaling.
BRING: AOE (Thunderclap, Whirlwind, Immolate+) for gremlin clearing, Shockwave+ (mass debuff), Disarm (reduce Leader Str). Non-exhaust damage cards for sustained fighting. Rage (0E power that generates block per attack -- excellent value in multi-attack turns). Potions -- this fight demands aggressive potion use. Fairy in a Bottle as safety net (but expect it to be consumed early if the fight goes long -- a Fairy consumed on turn 6 leaves no safety for turns 7-8 when Rally scaling peaks).
CORRUPTION + FNP ENGINE: Corruption makes Skills free, Feel No Pain generates block per exhaust. This engine is theoretically strong here but ONLY if it comes online by turn 2-3. If the engine activates on turn 7 of 8, Rally has already stacked to +9 Str on all enemies and incoming damage exceeds all available block. The engine needs early activation or it arrives too late.
STRENGTH SCALING IS MANDATORY: Without permanent Strength, you cannot kill the Leader fast enough. In run 98, Mutagenic Strength (+3 Str turn 1 only) enabled 35 damage to Leader on turn 1, but by turn 2 with 0 Str, damage output collapsed. The DPS race against Rally requires at least +2 permanent Strength (Inflame, Spot Weakness, Demon Form) or enough burst cards (Immolate + Heavy Blade + Bash+) to close the fight in 5 turns. Evaluate whether your deck can realistically kill 144 HP in 5-6 turns BEFORE entering this fight.
GREMLIN WIZARD PRIORITY: If Gremlin Wizard is present, it charges for a massive 28-damage nuke. Kill it before it fires. In run 98, Thunderclap+ (7 AOE + Vuln) into Pommel Strike+ (15 Vuln damage) = 22 damage exactly killed a 22 HP Wizard on turn 3, preventing 28 damage that would have been instantly fatal. Always check Wizard charge timer and prioritize killing it on the turn before it fires.
AVOID: Entering below 60% HP. Entering without potions. Entering without permanent Strength scaling. Exhaust-heavy decks (once Impervious/Shockwave/Disarm are gone, remaining deck is too weak). If below 50% HP, take any alternative path. Decks with reduced Max HP (e.g., post-Vampires at 56 Max HP) have no margin for error against this fight's scaling. Wasting turns -- do NOT batch `end` with draw effects like Battle Trance; the drawn cards need to be played that same turn.
