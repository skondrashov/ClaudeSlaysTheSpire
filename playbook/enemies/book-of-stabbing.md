# Book of Stabbing (elite, Act 2, HP: 161)
PATTERN: Multi-hit attacks that gain +1 hit every turn AND gain +3 Str every turn. The double scaling (more hits AND more damage per hit) means incoming damage grows quadratically, not linearly. Each attack adds Wound status cards (Unplayable, persist in deck).
KEY MECHANIC: Double scaling -- hit count increases by 1 every turn AND Str increases by +3 every turn. This means damage per turn grows much faster than a single scaling mechanic. Example observed (with Str 3 start): T1: 24, T2: 18 (with Piercing Wail Str reduction), T3: 27, T4: 36 (Weakened), T5: 30 (Weakened), T6: 54 (unmitigated). Without debuffs, raw damage escalates to 50+ by turn 5-6. Combined with Wound draw clog, this creates a rapid death spiral.

THE WOUND CLOG PROBLEM: By turn 6-7, Wounds can comprise 60%+ of the deck. A 21-card deck accumulating 3+ Wounds per turn means that by mid-fight, you draw 3 Wounds and 2 playable cards per turn. This makes it impossible to find block AND offense in the same hand. Strong cards like Double Tap, Reaper, Inflame become buried under Wounds. The fight becomes unwinnable not because of raw damage scaling but because the deck stops functioning.

WHAT THIS MEANS: Front-load damage -- every turn the fight goes on, attacks get worse AND Wounds clog draws. Apply Vulnerable immediately. Use exhaust tools for Wound removal. Enter with HIGH HP (60%+ minimum). Deck size matters: a 15-card deck survives Wound clog better than a 21-card deck because each Wound is a smaller percentage of draws. The +3 Str/turn means Weak and Piercing Wail are extremely valuable but temporary -- once they wear off, incoming damage jumps dramatically.

BENCHMARKS: Even with strong burst, expect 5+ turns. 140 damage dealt in 5 turns still left Book at 21 HP in one fight. At 6 HP entry, the fight is unwinnable. At 23 HP entry, the fight is unwinnable even with Demon Form scaling -- the escalating multi-hit outpaces block before Strength ramp matters. At 80 HP entry with a 21-card deck (no exhaust tools), HP went from 80 to 3 over 7 turns -- barely survivable even at full HP. At 33/70 (47%) with Dex 6 + Accuracy Shivs + Piercing Wail, dealt 137 damage in 6 turns but Book still had 24 HP when player died -- even strong offensive output falls short without exhaust tools or higher HP entry. Minimum entry: 60% HP with exhaust tools, or avoid entirely.

SILENT-SPECIFIC: Dex stacking alone (even Dex 6 from 2x Footwork+) cannot keep up with +3 Str/turn scaling. Piercing Wail buys one safe turn but exhausts. Weak from Neutralize+ reduces damage temporarily but the Str keeps growing. The Silent needs either: (1) burst damage through poison (Deadly Poison + Catalyst+ to front-load lethal poison), (2) Malaise to permanently reduce Str, or (3) avoid the fight entirely when lacking exhaust tools. Shiv decks (Accuracy + Blade Dance) provide decent damage per turn (~24-30) but cannot block the 30-54 incoming on turns 5-6. Without exhaust tools for Wounds, the Silent's small deck (18-19 cards) gets clogged faster than the Ironclad's typical 20+ card deck.

BRING: Exhaust tools for Wounds (True Grit+, Burning Pact, Second Wind, Fiend Fire; Silent: no native exhaust tools for Wounds) -- THIS IS THE #1 PRIORITY. Burst damage front-loaded in turns 1-3. Vulnerable application (Bash+; Silent: none available). Dropkick+ (free cycling when Vulnerable). Weak (reduces each individual hit -- critical given +3 Str/turn). Piercing Wail (Silent -- temporarily reduces all enemy Str, buys one critical safe turn). Malaise (Silent -- X-cost Str reduction that scales with energy, semi-permanent). Poison scaling (Silent -- Deadly Poison + Catalyst+ can front-load lethal poison that kills through block without needing to out-damage the Str scaling).

AVOID: Entering below 60% HP (confirmed: 47% entry with strong deck died on Turn 6). Entering with a large deck (20+ cards) and no exhaust. Slow play (each turn is worse than the last). Flex and other low-impact cards that dilute draws. Power-heavy setup (spending turns 1-3 on Footwork+/Accuracy/Tools of the Trade instead of dealing damage -- powers that don't deal damage cost precious early turns when incoming is still manageable).

DECK REQUIREMENTS: If your deck has no exhaust tools (True Grit+, Burning Pact, Second Wind, Fiend Fire), DO NOT fight Book of Stabbing voluntarily. The Wound clog WILL overwhelm any deck that cannot remove status cards from the draw pile. This is not a "nice to have" -- it is mandatory for decks above 15 cards.

BRIMSTONE INTERACTION (CRITICAL): If you have Brimstone, DO NOT fight Book of Stabbing. Brimstone gives Book +2 Str/turn on TOP of its native +3 Str/turn, for a combined +5 Str/turn. By turn 3, each hit is devastating. By turn 5, 6+ hits at 17+ damage each = 100+ total incoming. One confirmed death with Brimstone + strong player Str scaling (Inflame+ + Spot Weakness+ + Mutagenic Strength) at 57 HP entry -- died turn 5. The player's own Strength was excellent but could not outrace Book's Brimstone-boosted growth. This is a hard avoid -- no amount of Strength scaling compensates when the enemy's damage grows with both more hits AND more damage per hit.

TURN EFFICIENCY: Do not waste turns on setup that does not deal damage or provide meaningful block. One Ironclad death saw turn 2 spent on Armaments+ (5 block + upgrade hand) instead of damage -- that lost damage turn meant Book reached turn 5 alive with 53 HP remaining. One Silent death saw turns 1-3 spent on Accuracy + 2x Footwork+ + TotT (all Powers, zero immediate damage output) -- by the time damage started flowing, the Str scaling had already pushed incoming past blockable thresholds. Every turn costs approximately +7 more incoming damage than the previous turn (more with Brimstone, more with +3 Str/turn). Prioritize damage cards over utility/Power cards in this fight.
