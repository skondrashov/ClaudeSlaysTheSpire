# The Transient (Act 3 Hallway, HP: 999, Fading 5)

The Transient disappears after 5 turns (Fading 5 loses 1 per turn, dies at 0). It attacks with escalating damage every turn.

PATTERN: Attacks every turn with escalating damage.
- **Turn 1**: 30 damage (40 at A2)
- **Turn 2**: 40 damage (50 at A2)
- **Turn 3**: 50 damage (60 at A2)
- **Turn 4**: 60 damage (70 at A2)
- **Turn 5**: 70 damage (80 at A2)
- **Total damage over 5 turns: 250 at A0.** Damage increases by 10 per turn.

POWERS:
- **Fading 5**: Loses 1 per turn, dies at 0 (6 at A17). The Transient attacks BEFORE fading — you must survive every hit including the last.
- **Shifting**: When the Transient takes damage, it LOSES that much Strength for the rest of the turn. **This means attacking REDUCES its damage this turn.** If the Transient would deal 50 and you deal 20 damage to it, it only hits for 30. This is the single most important mechanic in the fight.

## STRATEGY — ATTACK TO SURVIVE

**The Transient fight is about dealing damage to reduce incoming damage.** Every point of damage you deal reduces the Transient's attack by the same amount this turn (via Shifting). This is far more efficient than blocking:
- 1 energy on a Strike (6 damage) = 6 less incoming damage = effectively 6 block
- 1 energy on a Defend (5 block) = 5 block
- **Attacking is strictly better than blocking** in this fight because damage dealt via Shifting reduces the attack AND you still get to play block cards with remaining energy.

**Optimal play each turn:**
1. Play all attack cards first — each point of damage reduces this turn's incoming
2. Play block cards with remaining energy to cover whatever damage is left
3. The combination of Shifting + block should get you to zero or near-zero damage taken

**Strength debuffs are excellent.** Disarm, Dark Shackles, Malaise — permanent or large temporary Strength reduction compounds across turns.

**Weak is excellent.** Reduces the already-Shifting-reduced damage by another 25%.

**Impervious is best saved for turns 3-5** when incoming is 50-70 base. Turn 1 (30) is manageable with normal cards.

## WHAT NOT TO DO
- **Do NOT play purely defensively.** Blocking without attacking wastes the Shifting mechanic and makes the fight much harder.
- Enter below 40% HP without strong block density or Strength debuffs.
- Exhaust key block cards in prior fights when Transient is upcoming.

## CHARACTER-SPECIFIC NOTES

**Defect:** Orb damage counts for Shifting. Lightning orb evokes and passive procs reduce the Transient's Strength. Frost orbs provide block. A balanced orb setup (Lightning + Frost) handles this fight well. Barrage with multiple orbs deals massive Shifting-reduced damage.

**Ironclad:** Impervious covers one turn completely. Barricade lets block accumulate. Disarm is permanent Strength reduction. Reaper can heal back chip damage.

**Silent:** Wraith Form+ gives 3 turns of Intangible (1 damage each). Piercing Wail reduces attack. Malaise+ is permanent Strength reduction. After Image+ provides passive block per card played. Poison does NOT help — the Transient fades on its own.
