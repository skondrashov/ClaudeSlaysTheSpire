# Red Slaver (hallway, Act 1, HP: 46-50)

PATTERN: Always starts with Stab. Then repeats Scrape -> Scrape -> Stab until Entangle is used. Has 25% chance per turn to use Entangle instead of the normal pattern. After Entangle (used only once per combat): 55% Stab, 45% Scrape (cannot use same move 3x in a row).

KEY MECHANIC: Entangle -- prevents playing ALL Attack cards for one turn. The only source of Entangled in the game. Can only be used once per combat.

DAMAGE:
- Stab: 13 damage (14 at A2)
- Scrape: 8 damage + 1 Vulnerable (9 + 1 Vuln at A2, 9 + 2 Vuln at A17)
- Entangle: Applies Entangled (cannot play Attack cards this turn)

DEBUFFS: Vulnerable (from Scrape), Entangled (from Entangle -- once per combat)

WHAT THIS MEANS: Entangle can be devastating if it hits on a turn where you need to deal damage. After Entangle, you can only play Skills and Powers. Plan for this by having block Skills ready. Scrape's Vulnerable makes subsequent attacks hit harder.

STRATEGY:
- Apply Vulnerable, burst down. 3-4 turn kill typical
- Block on Stab turns (13 damage)
- When Entangled: play only Skills (Defend, block cards). Do NOT waste the turn -- use it for setup
- Kill before Entangle if possible (25% chance per turn means it usually fires by turn 3-4)

DANGER LEVEL: Low-Medium. Entangle is annoying but not lethal for decks with adequate block cards.

APPEARS WITH: Solo in Act 1. With Blue Slaver + Taskmaster in Act 2 elite Slavers fight.
