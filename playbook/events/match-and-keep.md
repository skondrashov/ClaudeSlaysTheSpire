# Match and Keep (Act 2)

A card-matching memory game. Cards are face-down in pairs. Flip two at a time -- if they match, you add that card to your deck. If not, they flip back. You get a limited number of attempts.

OPTIONS:
- [Play] Play the matching game and obtain matched cards.
- [Leave] Skip the event. Nothing happens.

DECISION FRAMEWORK: The matched cards are added to your deck whether you want them or not. You cannot choose which pairs to match -- you match what you find. This means the event can ADD UNWANTED CARDS to your deck (observed: Bash and Perfected Strike added to a deck that did not want them). Deck dilution from bad matches is a real downside.

WHEN TO PLAY: Only when the deck is small enough that a few mediocre additions are tolerable, or when you are desperate for any card quality. If the deck is already well-tuned and lean, the risk of dilution outweighs the chance of finding a good card.

WHEN TO LEAVE: When the deck is already at a good size (14-18 cards) and adding random cards would dilute draws of key cards. This is the safer default choice.

WARNING: Unlike card rewards where you can skip, matched cards are automatically added to your deck. There is no way to refuse a match once made. This makes the event a gamble -- potentially helpful if you match a strong card, but actively harmful if you match mediocre ones like extra Strikes, Bash, or Perfected Strike.
