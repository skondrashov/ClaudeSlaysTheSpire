# Mark of Pain (Boss Relic)
Gain 1 additional energy each turn (3E -> 4E). Whenever you play an Attack, add a Wound to your draw pile.

EVALUATION: Strong boss relic. 4E per turn is a massive upgrade. The Wound downside is manageable and can become an advantage with the right cards. Wounds are Status cards that clog the draw pile, reducing hand quality by taking up draw slots. However, with Evolve/Evolve+, each Wound drawn triggers additional draws, converting the downside into card advantage.

SYNERGIES:
- **Evolve+ (BEST synergy)**: Each Wound drawn triggers 2 additional draws. This means playing attacks generates future card advantage. The 4E + bonus draws creates a dominant engine: 5-7 meaningful cards played per turn is typical. This is the strongest Mark of Pain synergy -- if Evolve+ is already in the deck, Mark of Pain is the clear best boss relic choice.
- **Evolve (unupgraded)**: Each Wound drawn triggers 1 additional draw. Still good but less dominant. Upgrade Evolve to Evolve+ as soon as possible after taking Mark of Pain.
- **Fire Breathing**: Wounds drawn trigger Fire Breathing damage to all enemies. Each attack played eventually generates 3-10 free damage.
- **Second Wind**: Exhausts all non-Attack cards in hand, including Wounds, for 5 block each. Clears Wound clog while generating block.
- **True Grit+**: Chosen exhaust can target Wounds specifically.

ANTI-SYNERGIES:
- **No Evolve/Status management**: Without Evolve or similar draw effects, Wounds progressively clog the draw pile. By turn 4-5, drawing 2-3 Wounds per hand is common, reducing effective hand size to 2-3 real cards. The 4E is wasted on a hand full of Wounds.
- **Battle Trance**: "Cannot draw additional cards this turn" prevents Evolve from triggering on Wound draws the turn Battle Trance is played.

WHEN TO TAKE: Take over Pandora's Box (too much variance) and Coffee Dripper (losing rest sites is dangerous without healing cards) when Evolve is already in the deck. If no Evolve exists, evaluate whether Wound clog will be manageable based on deck size and draw quality. A 15+ card deck dilutes Wounds better than a 10-card deck.

WHEN TO AVOID: If the deck has no way to manage Wounds (no Evolve, no Fire Breathing, no Second Wind, no True Grit+) and is small (under 12 cards). In this case, Wound clog will reduce hand quality faster than the extra energy compensates.
