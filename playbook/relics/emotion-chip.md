# Emotion Chip (Uncommon Relic, Defect-only)

EFFECT: If you lost HP during the previous turn, trigger the passive ability of all orbs at the start of your next turn (in addition to the normal end-of-turn passive trigger).

WHY IT'S STRONG: Effectively doubles orb passive output on turns after taking damage. With 3 Frost orbs at Focus 2, the normal end-of-turn passive is 12 block. With Emotion Chip triggering at the start of the next turn, that is an additional 12 block before the player even plays cards. Lightning orbs deal their passive damage twice (6 damage per orb per cycle instead of 3).

KEY INTERACTIONS:
- **Frost orbs + Focus:** The defensive ceiling is enormous. With 5 Frost orbs at Focus 3, Emotion Chip adds 25 block at the start of the turn (5 orbs x 5 block each). This stacks with the normal 25 block at end of turn for 50 passive block per cycle.
- **Lightning orbs:** Double passive damage. Less impactful than Frost doubling because Lightning passives hit random enemies, but still meaningful in multi-enemy fights.
- **Dark orbs:** Dark orb passive gains (6+Focus) damage each turn. Emotion Chip triggers the passive gain twice, accelerating Dark orb scaling.
- **Self-damage effects:** Cards or relics that cause self-damage (e.g., Brutality) technically trigger Emotion Chip because they cause HP loss. However, deliberately taking damage to trigger Emotion Chip is rarely worth it -- the orb passives need to be substantial to justify the HP cost.

DECISION FRAMEWORK: Emotion Chip is strongest in Frost-heavy builds with Focus scaling. It rewards taking chip damage (which is often unavoidable) by converting it into defensive value the following turn. In a deck that reliably channels 3+ Frost orbs and has Focus 2+, Emotion Chip provides significant free block that compounds the Defect's defensive engine.

LIMITATION: Only triggers if HP was lost the previous turn. In fights where the player takes zero damage (full block discipline), Emotion Chip provides no value. It is a recovery tool, not a proactive one.
