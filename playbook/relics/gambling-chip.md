# Gambling Chip (uncommon relic)

At the start of each combat, discard any number of cards from your opening hand and draw that many new cards.

WHEN IT'S GOOD: Every combat. The mulligan effect lets you find key cards (Bash+, Inflame+, Shockwave, Whirlwind) on Turn 1. With a 19-card deck and a 5-card opening hand, discarding 2-3 weak cards and drawing replacements dramatically increases the chance of having the right tool for the fight.

DECISION POINTS: What to discard depends entirely on the fight:
- **Gremlin Leader**: Discard block cards (Defend, Shrug It Off), keep attacks. Turn economy demands damage from Turn 1.
- **Spheric Guardian**: Discard block cards, keep Artifact strippers (Thunderclap, Shockwave) and attacks. This is a DPS check.
- **Hallway fights**: Discard expensive Powers (Barricade, Demon Form) and situational exhausts (Shockwave for single enemies). Keep Bash+ and cheap attacks.
- **Chosen/Hex fights**: Discard Skills (they trigger Hex adding Dazed). Keep Attacks and Powers.
- **Boss fights**: Discard based on boss pattern. Guardian Turn 1 is usually DEFEND -- discard block, keep setup cards.

SYNERGIES: Lean decks (15-17 cards) -- fewer cards means the mulligan is more likely to find specific answers. Cards with fight-specific value (Shockwave, Intimidate, Barricade) can be safely included in the deck because they can be discarded in fights where they're not needed.

VALUE: High. Consistently useful in every combat. One of the best uncommon relics for deck consistency.
