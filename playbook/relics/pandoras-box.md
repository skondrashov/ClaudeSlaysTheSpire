# Pandora's Box (Boss Relic)
EFFECT: Transform ALL Strike and Defend cards into random cards.

VALUE: Extremely high variance. Removes 10 starter cards and replaces them with random class cards. Can produce game-winning decks or unplayable ones. The outcome is entirely luck-dependent and cannot be evaluated before accepting.

CRITICAL RISK -- BLOCK LOSS: Pandora's Box removes ALL 4 Defend cards. If the random replacements contain zero block cards, the deck has NO defensive capability for all of Act 1. This is not a gap that can be easily patched: shops offer limited card selection, and card rewards are random. In Run 103, Pandora's Box produced an offense-heavy deck (Offering, Carnage, Barricade, Rampage, Perfected Strike x2, etc.) with zero block cards. Despite buying Iron Wave and Flame Barrier at shops, maximum block per turn was ~10-12, which is catastrophically low for the Guardian's 32-damage attack. The run died on Floor 16 to Guardian with no ability to block sustained damage.

WHEN TO TAKE IT:
- From Neow's boss relic swap: HIGH RISK. You see the result immediately but cannot undo it. If the transformations produce no block cards, the run may be unwinnable against Guardian or Hexaghost. Only take if you are comfortable gambling the entire run.
- As a boss reward after Act 1: Less risky because the deck already has non-starter cards that are unaffected, and Act 2 shops/rewards can fill gaps.

AFTER TAKING IT:
- Immediately assess: does the new deck have ANY block source? Count cards that provide block (Defend replacements, Iron Wave, Shrug It Off, Flame Barrier, True Grit, etc.).
- If zero block cards: this is an EMERGENCY. Prioritize acquiring block at the very next shop or card reward. Iron Wave (1E, 5/5 dual purpose) is the minimum viable option. Flame Barrier (2E, 12 block + counter) is better.
- Disarm and Dark Shackles reduce enemy damage but are not substitutes for block cards in long fights.
- Feel No Pain provides exhaust-based block but only triggers 3-6 times per fight -- not enough for sustained defense.
- Barricade (3E) is too slow to set up in fights with early pressure (Guardian, Hexaghost first attacks).

DEFECT-SPECIFIC NOTE: The Defect's starter deck has 10 replaceable cards (4 Strikes, 4 Defends, Zap, Dualcast). Pandora's Box replaces all of them with random Defect cards. Losing Zap and Dualcast is low-cost (they are weak). Losing Cracked Core (starter relic) does NOT happen -- Pandora's Box replaces the starter relic, not individual cards. In the first Defect victory, Pandora's Box Neow swap replaced Cracked Core with Pandora's Box, transforming the starter deck into a much stronger starting pool that included Coolheaded, Cold Snap, Sweeping Beam, Claw, and other Defect cards. The loss of Cracked Core's Turn 1 Lightning orb was irrelevant because the deck generated orbs through cards immediately.

DECISION FRAMEWORK: Pandora's Box from Neow is a run-defining gamble. The upside (10 powerful cards replacing 10 weak starters) is enormous but the downside (no block = dead at Act 1 boss) is terminal. Prefer safer Neow options (100 Gold, Remove 2 Cards) unless you have no other path to a strong deck. For the Defect specifically, Pandora's Box is slightly safer than for other classes because the Defect's starter cards are weaker (Zap and Dualcast are not impactful), and the random Defect card pool includes many strong orb-channeling cards.
