# Busted Crown (Boss Relic)

EFFECT: Gain 1 Energy at the start of each turn (+1E, total 4E). Card reward choices reduced by 2.

WHY IT'S GOOD: Going from 3E to 4E is transformative. Every turn plays an extra card -- that is 5 more block (Defend) or 6+ more damage (Strike) per turn. On Turn 1, the extra energy enables Bash+ (2E) + Defend (1E) + another card (1E), which is impossible at 3E. Against Bronze Automaton, the extra energy enabled playing more block cards alongside damage, which was critical for surviving Hyper Beam.

DOWNSIDE: Card reward choices reduced from 3 to 1. This makes it harder to find specific cards (Reaper, Impervious) from card rewards. The downside matters less when the deck already has its core cards by Floor 15, and matters more early in Act 1 when every card reward is critical for deck building.

WHEN TO TAKE: Take when the deck's core cards are already acquired (by Act 1 boss reward). The extra energy helps play those cards more effectively. In the first victory, Busted Crown was taken as the Act 1 boss relic because the deck already had Immolate+, Bash+, Spot Weakness+, Rampage, Impervious, Evolve, Shrug It Off, and Cleave -- all core cards.

WHEN TO AVOID: If the deck is still missing critical cards (healing, block scaling) and needs card rewards to find them. The 2 fewer choices makes RNG denial worse. If Snecko Eye is also being considered for the same slot, Busted Crown loses value since Snecko Eye provides its own draw advantage.

NOTE: In the first victory, Busted Crown was the Act 1 boss relic (4E for Acts 1-2) and Snecko Eye was the Act 2 boss relic (7 draws for Act 3). The combination was extremely powerful because Snecko Eye's extra draws made the reduced card rewards from Busted Crown irrelevant.

EVALUATION: Strong boss relic. The +1E is reliable every turn and immediately impactful. The card reward reduction is a real cost but can be mitigated by shops and Dream Catcher (card rewards from rest sites).
