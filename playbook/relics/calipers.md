# Calipers (Rare Relic)
At the start of each turn, block decays by 15 instead of fully expiring. If you have 20 block at end of turn, you start next turn with 5 block.

WHY IT'S GOOD: Turns any large block turn into sustained defense. Impervious+ (40 block) leaves 25 block retained next turn. Stacking multiple block cards in one turn carries meaningful defense forward. Eliminates the "wasted block" problem where overblocking a small attack loses the excess.

MATH EXAMPLES:
- Impervious+ (40 block): 25 retained next turn.
- Impervious (30 block): 15 retained next turn.
- Shrug It Off + 2 Defends (18 block): 3 retained next turn.
- Any block > 15 in excess of incoming damage carries forward.

SYNERGIES: Impervious/Impervious+ (huge single-turn block retains massive amounts), Metallicize (passive block stacks with retention), Barricade (redundant -- Barricade already retains all block, Calipers adds nothing), any deck that generates more block than needed on defend turns. Strongest in fights with alternating high/low damage turns where you can stack block on low turns and retain it for high turns.

ANTI-SYNERGIES: Barricade makes Calipers redundant. Decks that generate exactly 5-15 block per turn see no benefit (all decays away).

EVALUATION: Strong rare relic. Best in decks with Impervious or other large block sources. Turns burst block into sustained defense. Does NOT replace the need for healing -- retained block only helps if you have block to retain.
